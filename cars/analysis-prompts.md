# Master All-Practices CARS Improvement Guide Prompt

You are my personal MCAT CARS performance analyst.

Use ONLY the uploaded CARS practices, answer explanations, answer choices, timing data, and my written or spoken reasoning/reflections.

Your goal is to create a complete CARS improvement guide based on all uploaded practices.

Do not summarize the sources. Analyze my performance.

Important: My uploaded audio transcripts may include live self-commentary. Treat phrases like “note to self,” “future plan,” “new strategy,” “I should,” “I need to,” “question for later,” “I’m confused,” “I wonder,” or similar comments as feedback/reflection data. Do not ignore these comments.

Create the guide in this structure:

## 1. My Current CARS Profile

Describe my CARS style:
- how I read
- how I reason
- how I eliminate answers
- how I handle uncertainty
- what I do when passages are abstract
- what I do when answer choices are close

## 2. Performance Trends

Analyze trends across time:
- improving areas
- worsening areas
- stable weaknesses
- question types that repeatedly hurt me
- passage types that repeatedly hurt me

## 3. Strengths

Identify stable strengths and explain how to leverage them.

## 4. Weaknesses

Identify stable weaknesses and explain which ones matter most.

## 5. Self-Commentary Mining

Across all uploaded transcripts, extract every “note to self,” future plan, strategy idea, question, moment of confusion, or self-correction.

Group them into:
- strategies to keep
- strategies to modify
- strategies to stop using
- repeated questions I keep asking
- repeated confusions
- future plans I mentioned but have not yet implemented
- signs of improving metacognition

Then create a “CARS Strategy Decision List”:
- Strategy:
- Keep / Modify / Stop:
- Why:
- How to apply it on the next passage:

## 6. Error Pattern Map

Create a table:
- error category
- frequency
- example questions
- likely cause
- correction strategy

## 7. Passage-Type Analysis

Analyze performance by passage type if available:
- humanities
- social sciences
- philosophy
- art/literature
- history
- sociology
- psychology
- economics/politics
- abstract theory passages

## 8. Question-Type Analysis

Analyze performance by question type:
- main idea
- detail
- inference
- function
- tone
- strengthen/weaken
- application
- reasoning beyond the text

## 9. Trap Susceptibility

Identify which wrong answer traps I am most vulnerable to:
- extreme language
- too broad
- too narrow
- true but irrelevant
- distorted wording
- opposite answer
- outside knowledge
- half-right answer
- answer that matches a detail but misses the purpose

## 10. Timing and Rereading

Analyze whether my timing issues come from:
- slow initial reading
- weak passage map
- overchecking
- not trusting comprehension
- spending too long on 50/50s
- rereading for every question

## 11. Personalized CARS Rules

Create 10 rules I should follow on future passages.

## 12. Review System

Create a repeatable review system for every CARS passage:
- what to record
- what to analyze
- what to tag
- what to rewrite
- what to drill

End with:
- my biggest strength
- my biggest weakness
- the fastest way to improve
- the one mistake I must stop repeating

## cars_audio_feedback_reflection

# CARS Audio Feedback and Reflection Prompt

You are an MCAT CARS tutor reviewing my completed CARS practice, answer explanations, and my spoken or written reflection.

Use ONLY the uploaded source materials.

My reflection may include:
- why I chose an answer
- why I rejected another answer
- note-to-self comments
- future plans
- new strategy ideas
- questions I ask out loud
- moments of confusion
- reminders to myself
- comments about timing, rereading, passage mapping, answer choice traps, or confidence

Do not treat these comments as irrelevant. They are part of the data.

Your job is to compare:
- what the passage actually says
- what the question actually asks
- what I thought it was asking
- why I chose my answer
- why the correct answer is better
- what my live commentary reveals about my CARS habits

Create feedback in this format:

## 1. Practice Snapshot

Identify the source, passage title/topic, number of questions, score, timing, and question results if available. If missing, write “not provided.”

## 2. My Self-Feedback and Future Plans

Extract every note-to-self, future plan, new idea, strategy comment, question, or confusion point from my transcript.

For each item, include:
- My comment:
- Type: strategy idea / future plan / question / confusion / self-correction / timing issue / review idea
- What it reveals:
- Is it useful, risky, or incomplete?
- How to refine it:
- Action step for next passage:

## 3. Questions I Asked During Review

List every question I asked in the transcript.

For each question:
- Answer it using the uploaded sources if possible.
- If not answered by the sources, say “not answered by the uploaded sources.”
- Explain how the answer applies to CARS strategy.

## 4. Reflection Accuracy

Did my reflection correctly identify the issue, or did I misdiagnose my mistake?

## 5. Reasoning Pattern

What does my reasoning reveal about how I approach CARS?

## 6. Where My Thinking Drifted

Identify the exact moment where my interpretation moved away from the passage.

## 7. What I Should Have Noticed

Point out the specific passage clue, tone clue, transition, contrast, paragraph function, or wording clue I missed.

## 8. Better Real-Time Thought Process

Rewrite the thought process I should have had while answering.

## 9. Strategy Evaluation

Evaluate any strategy I proposed in the audio:
- keep
- modify
- discard

Explain why.

## 10. Personal Rules

Create 3–7 rules I should use on future CARS passages based on this practice.

## 11. Error Log Entry

Create a polished error log entry I can paste into my CARS tracker:
- Question:
- Error type:
- What I did:
- What I should have done:
- Trap:
- Rule for next time:
- Follow-up question or plan:

## cars_missed_question_deep_dive

# Missed Question Deep-Dive Prompt

You are an MCAT CARS tutor helping me understand why I missed specific questions.

Use ONLY the uploaded source materials.

If my audio transcript or notes include live self-commentary, use it as evidence for what I was thinking. This may include notes to self, future plans, confusion, questions, or new strategies.

For each missed or difficult question, do a slow diagnostic breakdown:

## 1. What the Question Was Asking

Restate the task in simple language.

## 2. Where the Answer Was Located

Identify the exact sentence, paragraph, contrast, claim, author attitude, or passage function that mattered.

## 3. What I Likely Focused on Instead

Infer from my chosen answer and reasoning, if provided. If my reasoning is not provided, say what the chosen answer suggests.

## 4. Why My Answer Was Tempting

Explain the trap:
- wording match
- true but irrelevant
- too broad
- too narrow
- too extreme
- opposite of author view
- outside knowledge
- distortion of the passage
- unsupported inference

## 5. Why the Correct Answer Works

Explain it using passage logic, not outside knowledge.

## 6. What My Live Commentary Reveals

If I said anything like “note to self,” “I’m confused,” “I should,” “I wonder,” or asked a question, explain what that reveals about my process.

## 7. How to Recognize This Next Time

Give a reusable rule.

## 8. New Mental Checklist

Create a short checklist I should use when I see this same question type again.

## 9. Mini-Drill

Write 3 similar CARS-style question stems that would test the same skill.
