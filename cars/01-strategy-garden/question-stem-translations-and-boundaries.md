# Question Stem Translations and Strategy Boundaries

## Purpose

Use this guide to translate CARS stems into plain English and avoid mixing strategies that belong to different question types.

> **Inference works forward. Assumption works backward.**

## Suggests

**Plain English:** What does the passage point toward?

Use:

- a small, supported inference
- the direction of the author's point
- the answer with the least extra assumption

Do not use:

- a direct-detail-only approach
- the assumption negation test
- a broad claim that merely sounds insightful

## Implies / Can reasonably be inferred

**Plain English:** Given what the passage says, what follows?

Use:

- one supported step beyond the text
- local context plus the passage's reasoning
- conservative wording

Do not use:

- outside knowledge
- strong certainty unsupported by the text
- a multi-step chain of speculation

## Assumes / Takes for granted

**Plain English:** What had to be true already for the argument to work?

Use:

- the hidden bridge between evidence and conclusion
- the negation test when helpful
- a necessary foundation rather than a merely relevant fact

Do not use:

- the inference question: “What follows from this?”
- a fact that would strengthen the argument but is not required

## Most likely agree

**Plain English:** Which statement best matches the author's view?

Use:

- author tone
- passage main idea
- the same relationship or principle

Do not use:

- topic matching alone
- a choice that is true in real life but not supported by the passage

## According to the passage

**Plain English:** What does the text directly support?

Use:

- exact line retrieval
- nearby context
- careful paraphrase

Do not use:

- broad main-idea reasoning when a direct line settles the question
- outside knowledge

## Based on the passage

**Plain English:** What answer is supported by the passage, possibly with a small inference?

Use:

- passage evidence
- a modest extension
- author logic

Do not use:

- a large leap
- an answer that only sounds plausible

## Why does the author mention / The role of

**Plain English:** What job does this sentence, quote, example, person, or paragraph perform?

Use:

- why it appears here
- what larger claim it supports, challenges, illustrates, or qualifies

Do not use:

- a content summary only
- a dictionary definition

## Most analogous

**Plain English:** Which new situation has the same underlying relationship?

Use:

- mechanism matching
- direction matching
- role matching

Do not use:

- same topic
- same setting
- same vocabulary without the same structure

## Strengthen

**Plain English:** Which answer makes the author's claim more believable?

Use:

1. identify the exact claim
2. identify the evidence-to-conclusion link
3. choose what supports that link

Do not use:

- a fact about a side example
- a statement that is merely consistent with the passage

## Weaken

**Plain English:** Which answer makes the author's claim less believable?

Use:

- attack the core claim
- break a required relationship
- introduce an alternative explanation when relevant

Do not use:

- evidence against an exaggerated claim the author never made
- evidence that only limits one example when the argument requires only some examples

## Validity of the argument / Most useful to know

**Plain English:** Which unanswered issue could most directly prove or disprove the central reasoning?

Use:

- the assumption beneath the argument
- the definition or distinction the argument depends on
- evidence that could change whether the conclusion follows

Do not use:

- background history
- a question about a named person who is only an example
- a limitation that does not threaten the actual claim

## Main idea / Central thesis

**Plain English:** What is the author's final point about the passage's main issue?

Use:

- passage-wide direction
- repeated claims
- the payoff or conclusion

Do not use:

- introductory setup alone
- one vivid example
- one quotation

## EXCEPT / Least / Inconsistent

**Plain English:** Find the answer that does not fit.

Use:

1. reverse the task explicitly
2. label three choices as supported/expected
3. select the odd one out

Do not use:

- the ordinary positive version of the question
- quick selection before confirming the negative word

## Vocabulary in context

**Plain English:** What does this word or phrase mean here?

Use:

- sentence context
- paragraph role
- substitution into the sentence

Do not use:

- dictionary definition alone
- familiar everyday meaning when the passage uses the term differently

## Scope words to watch

| Passage wording | Do not upgrade to |
|---|---|
| can | should, must, always, first |
| some | all |
| may | certainly |
| different | inferior, weaker, simpler |
| influences | determines completely |
| obscured | intentionally destroyed |
| under the same conditions | every person in every possible situation |

## Fast test-day translation

- **suggests** = points toward
- **implies** = one supported step
- **assumes** = hidden bridge
- **function** = why included
- **analogy** = same mechanism
- **strengthen/weaken** = help or hurt the exact link
- **according to** = direct support
- **based on** = support plus a small inference
- **EXCEPT/least/inconsistent** = find the one that does not fit
