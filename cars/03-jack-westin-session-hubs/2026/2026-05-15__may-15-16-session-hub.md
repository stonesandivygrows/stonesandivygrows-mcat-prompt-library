## Core Findings

### ★ Main Strategy Finding

Performance problems in this block were driven less by passage knowledge than by choosing the wrong processing mode under pressure.

### ⚠️ Main Contrast

The strongest answers were not always the ones with the most familiar wording. They were the ones that preserved the author’s exact relationship, scope, and purpose.

### → Repeating Failure Chain

Dense syntax or uncertainty
→ over-rereading or retrieval mode
→ loss of passage function
→ attraction to a familiar but structurally weaker answer
→ time pressure and reduced confidence

## Active Rules From This Session

### Keep

- Read in order rather than scanning the entire test when scanning increases overload.
- Use title/source only for light orientation.
- Track paragraph function and argumentative direction.
- Read silently; use finger anchoring only if it does not slow pace.
- Compare answer choices by verb, scope, and relationship.

### Modify

- Use last sentences as clues, not automatic summaries.
- Return to the passage when the stem requires local support, not by default.
- Strip syntax interruptions functionally; do not use “skip commas.”
- Tag skipped questions by reason and rely on navigation rather than memory.

### Stop

- Full-test passage-order scanning during standard practice.
- Written paragraph maps during timed passages.
- Treating inference, assumption, or function questions as direct retrieval.
- Choosing an answer because its words feel closer to the passage topic.

### Retest

- Three-layer passage formatting
- Mid-passage focus check after paragraph 2 or 3
- One-phrase skip-reason tags
- Bidirectional arrow check for reciprocal claims
