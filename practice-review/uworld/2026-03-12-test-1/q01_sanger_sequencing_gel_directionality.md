# Q1 Review — Sanger Sequencing + Gel Directionality

## Core concept

Sanger sequencing uses chain-terminating ddNTPs to create DNA fragments of different lengths. Gel electrophoresis separates those fragments by size, so the smallest fragments travel farthest down the gel.

## What ddNTP means

- dNTP = deoxynucleotide triphosphate
- ddNTP = dideoxynucleotide triphosphate
- The N is a placeholder for A, T, C, or G.
- Examples: ddATP, ddTTP, ddCTP, ddGTP.

## Why ddNTP stops DNA synthesis

Normal DNA synthesis requires the growing strand’s 3′-OH to attack the alpha phosphate of the incoming dNTP.

When a ddNTP is incorporated, it lacks a 3′-OH. Therefore, the next incoming nucleotide has no 3′-OH available at the growing strand end to form the next phosphodiester bond.

Sticky correction:

**ddNTP can be incorporated, but once incorporated, it lacks the 3′-OH needed for the next phosphodiester bond.**

## Pyrophosphate logic

During normal nucleotide addition:

1. The 3′-OH of the growing strand acts as a nucleophile.
2. It attacks the alpha phosphate of the incoming dNTP.
3. A new phosphodiester bond forms.
4. Pyrophosphate leaves.
5. Pyrophosphate hydrolysis helps drive the reaction forward by making the overall process more favorable.

Sticky correction:

**Energy for DNA synthesis comes from the triphosphate of the incoming dNTP, not from a thioester bond.**

## How to read a Sanger gel

- Smaller fragments are at the bottom.
- Larger fragments are near the top.
- Read bottom to top.
- Reading bottom to top gives the synthesized strand in the 5′ to 3′ direction.

Sticky correction:

**Sanger gel: read bottom → top = 5′ → 3′ synthesized strand.**

## When to complement

Do not automatically complement every sequence.

Use the wording of the question:

- If the question asks for the sequence represented by the gel, read the gel directly bottom to top.
- If the question asks for the template strand, complement the synthesized/read strand.
- If the question provides a sequence and asks which gel represents it, match the direct sequence unless it explicitly asks for the complement/template.

## Related confusion repaired

The test review clarified that DNA polymerase reads template 3′ to 5′ while synthesizing the new strand 5′ to 3′. The gel output is read as the synthesized sequence from shortest to longest fragment.
