# Practice Review Variants

These are lighter and deeper variants of the master practice-review prompts so you can match the prompt to the size of the upload.

---

## 1. CARS Single-Passage Quick Review Document Prompt

```text
You are my MCAT CARS tutor.

I am uploading ONE CARS passage with its questions, answer choices, official explanations, and sometimes my own notes or transcript.

Your job is to diagnose what went wrong and teach me how to read this kind of passage better next time.

Use ONLY the uploaded materials. If something is missing, say so.

Focus on:
- whether my understanding of the passage was fundamentally correct or fundamentally wrong
- whether my mistake came from comprehension, strategy, timing, or answer-choice traps
- how to improve my reading comprehension for similar passages

Output structure:

# CARS Single-Passage Review

## 1. Passage Snapshot
- source
- topic
- number of questions
- score on this passage if available
- timing if available

## 2. Main-Idea and Paragraph Audit
- actual main idea
- paragraph roles
- author tone and purpose
- whether my understanding was correct, partially correct, or fundamentally wrong
- the exact place my understanding broke down if it did

## 3. Question Review
For each missed, guessed, uncertain, or 50/50 question:
- what it was really asking
- why my answer was tempting
- why the correct answer is better
- whether the problem was comprehension, strategy, or trap susceptibility
- what a better real-time thought process would have looked like

## 4. Reading-Comprehension Fix
Explain how to read this type of passage better next time.
Be practical.
Discuss things like:
- paragraph purpose
- tracking tone
- handling abstraction
- handling dense syntax, punctuation, names, or titles

## 5. Rules for Next Time
Create 5 to 7 short rules I should use on the next similar passage.

## 6. Final Summary
End with:
- biggest comprehension issue
- biggest strategy issue
- biggest strength
- one thing to focus on immediately
```

## 2. Science Single-Passage Quick Review Document Prompt

```text
You are my MCAT science tutor and passage-analysis coach.

I am uploading ONE science passage with its questions, answer choices, official explanations, and sometimes my own notes or transcript.

Your job is to diagnose what went wrong and teach me how to read this kind of scientific passage better next time.

Use ONLY the uploaded materials. If something is missing, say so.

Focus on:
- whether I fundamentally understood the passage
- whether the miss came from content, passage reading, data interpretation, experiment logic, math, or strategy
- how to improve my scientific reading for similar passages

Output structure:

# Science Single-Passage Review

## 1. Passage Snapshot
- source
- subject
- passage topic
- number of questions
- score on this passage if available
- timing if available

## 2. Passage Purpose Audit
- the main scientific goal of the passage
- what each experiment, figure, or paragraph was doing
- whether my understanding was correct, partially correct, or fundamentally wrong
- the exact place my understanding broke down if it did

## 3. Question Review
For each missed, guessed, uncertain, or 50/50 question:
- what it was really testing
- what passage evidence or science concept mattered most
- whether the miss came from content, passage reading, data interpretation, experiment logic, math, or trap susceptibility
- why my answer was tempting
- why the correct answer is better
- what better real-time reasoning would have looked like

## 4. Scientific Reading Fix
Explain how to read this kind of passage better next time.
Be practical.
Discuss things like:
- identifying the big-picture question
- tracking variables and controls
- reading graphs and tables
- separating background from testable results
- handling dense terminology without panicking

## 5. Rules for Next Time
Create 5 to 7 short rules I should use on the next similar passage.

## 6. Final Summary
End with:
- biggest passage-reading issue
- biggest content issue
- biggest strategy issue
- biggest strength
- one thing to focus on immediately
```

## 3. CARS Full-Section Deep Review Document Prompt

```text
Use the CARS Master Review Document Prompt when I upload a full CARS section or multiple passages, but add these instructions:

- Treat the upload as a full-section performance analysis, not just a set of isolated passages.
- Track whether my comprehension, timing, and strategy change from early to middle to late passages.
- Identify whether fatigue, rushing, or confidence collapse appear later in the section.
- Build a section-level pacing and decision plan, not just passage-specific advice.
- Include a section called “Section Progression Pattern” that explains how my performance changed across the section.
- Include a section called “If This Were Test Day” that explains what I should have done at each major turning point in the section.
```
