Blood pressure can be measured in the brachial artery, which has a cross-sectional area of 25 mm2 in a healthy adult. The blood flow rate is related to the power the person generates to move their body, as shown Figure 1.

Figure 1 Relationship between the brachial artery blood flow rate of a healthy adult and the power generated for body motion

 

An athlete running on a horizontal treadmill maintained a brachial artery blood flow rate of 1.1 L/min for 30 s. The treadmill had an electric circuit used to charge a parallel-plate capacitor of capacitance 1 mF.

The maximum and minimum blood pressures of a healthy adult (the systolic Ps and the diastolic Pd pressures, respectively) are about Ps  = 120 mmHg and Pd = 75 mm Hg. The mean arterial pressure PMPA is given by

PMPA = Pd + (Ps – Pd)/3

Equation 1

and the pulse pressure Pp is defined as

Pp = Ps – Pd

Equation 2

 

Ca2+ ions are normally found in the blood at a concentration of 100 mg/L. The location of internal organs where changes in the calcium content occur can be found by placing positron-emitting isotopes such as 15O (with a half-life T1/2 = 2 min) into the patient’s bloodstream. When positrons annihilate with electrons, they produce pairs of gamma photons, each with an energy of 500 keV. The location where gamma photons originate gives the location of the organ of interest.

 

 

Question

Passage 4 (Questions 18-21)

Blood pressure can be measured in the brachial artery, which has a cross-sectional area of 25 mm2 in a healthy adult. The blood flow rate is related to the power the person generates to move their body, as shown Figure 1.

Figure 1 Relationship between the brachial artery blood flow rate of a healthy adult and the power generated for body motion

 

An athlete running on a horizontal treadmill maintained a brachial artery blood flow rate of 1.1 L/min for 30 s. The treadmill had an electric circuit used to charge a parallel-plate capacitor of capacitance 1 mF.

The maximum and minimum blood pressures of a healthy adult (the systolic Ps and the diastolic Pd pressures, respectively) are about Ps  = 120 mmHg and Pd = 75 mm Hg. The mean arterial pressure PMPA is given by

PMPA = Pd + (Ps – Pd)/3

Equation 1

and the pulse pressure Pp is defined as

Pp = Ps – Pd

Equation 2

 

Ca2+ ions are normally found in the blood at a concentration of 100 mg/L. The location of internal organs where changes in the calcium content occur can be found by placing positron-emitting isotopes such as 15O (with a half-life T1/2 = 2 min) into the patient’s bloodstream. When positrons annihilate with electrons, they produce pairs of gamma photons, each with an energy of 500 keV. The location where gamma photons originate gives the location of the organ of interest.

 

 

What fraction of a 15O sample decays in 10 min?

  • A.
    1/8
  • B.
    9/16
  • C.
    3/4
  • D.
    31/32

Solution: The correct answer is D.

  1. Based on the radioactive decay law N(t) = N (1/2)t/T where T = 2 min, this fraction of 15O decays in t = 2 min × ln(7/8) ÷ ln(1/2) = 22 seconds.
  2. Based on the radioactive decay law N(t) = N (1/2)t/T where T = 2 min, this fraction of 15O decays in t = 2 min × ln(7/16) ÷ ln(1/2) = 2.4 minutes.
  3. Based on the radioactive decay law N(t) = N (1/2)t/T where T = 2 min, this fraction of 15O decays in t = 2 min × ln(1/4) ÷ ln(1/2) = 4 minutes.
  4. This answer is correct because based on the radioactive decay law N(t) = N (1/2)t/T where T = 2 min, this fraction of 15O decays in t = 2 min × ln(1/32) ÷ ln(1/2) = 10 minutes.
