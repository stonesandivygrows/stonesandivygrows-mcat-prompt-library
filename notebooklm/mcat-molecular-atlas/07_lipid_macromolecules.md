# NotebookLM Prompt — Lipid Macromolecules

```text
You are creating an MCAT tutor-style audio overview for the student.

Topic family:
Lipid macromolecules across C/P, B/B, and Behavioral Science when relevant.

Goal:
Teach lipid categories as separate but related molecule families. The student should be able to distinguish fatty acids, triacylglycerols, phospholipids, cholesterol, steroid hormones, eicosanoids, and membrane lipids by structure and function.

Do not include CARS. Do not turn this into a full digestive system, endocrine system, or body systems lecture. Focus on molecular recognition, chemical logic, function, MCAT passage contexts, and traps.

Use Kaplan and UWorld materials as the factual foundation.

Teaching style:
Speak like two calm MCAT tutors. Be slow, visual, and concrete. Teach by “hydrophobic scaffold → functional group/polar head → biological role.” Do not just list facts.

Required sections:

1. What lipids are
Explain lipids as mostly hydrophobic or amphipathic molecules. Clarify that lipids are grouped by solubility/behavior more than by one single repeated monomer.

2. Fatty acids
Explain saturated vs unsaturated, cis/trans, chain length, carboxylic acid head, hydrophobic tail, omega notation only if relevant, and energy storage logic.

3. Triacylglycerols
Explain glycerol backbone plus three fatty acids, ester bonds, neutral fat, and energy storage role.

4. Phospholipids and membranes
Explain glycerophospholipids, phosphate-containing polar head, fatty acid tails, amphipathic structure, bilayers, and membrane fluidity.

5. Cholesterol and steroids
Explain cholesterol as a sterol with fused rings, membrane fluidity role, and precursor logic for steroid hormones. Clarify cholesterol vs steroid hormone vs bile salts when relevant.

6. Signaling lipids
Explain eicosanoids, prostaglandins, and lipid-derived signaling molecules only at MCAT depth.

7. Other high-yield lipid families
Include sphingolipids, waxes, fat-soluble vitamins, ketone bodies, and lipid transport only if supported by the uploaded sources.

For each lipid or term, include:
1. What it is in plain English
2. What molecular family it belongs to
3. What structure/scaffold to recognize
4. What changed compared with related molecules
5. What function that change creates
6. Where it appears across C/P, B/B, and P/S when relevant
7. Common MCAT passage contexts
8. Common answer-choice traps
9. Similar-sounding or similar-looking terms
10. One reusable takeaway

Required comparisons:
- fatty acid vs triacylglycerol
- phospholipid vs triglyceride
- saturated vs unsaturated fatty acid
- cis vs trans unsaturated fatty acid
- cholesterol vs steroid hormone
- hydrophobic vs amphipathic
- ester bond vs ether bond if relevant
- beta-oxidation vs ketogenesis vs fatty acid synthesis only as recognition, not a full pathway lecture

End with:
1. Top recognition patterns
2. Top confusing pairs
3. Highest-yield MCAT traps
4. A 10-line cheat sheet the student can memorize
```
