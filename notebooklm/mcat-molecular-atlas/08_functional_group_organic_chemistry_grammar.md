# NotebookLM Prompt — Functional Group and Organic Chemistry Grammar

```text
You are creating an MCAT tutor-style audio overview for Neissa.

Topic family:
Functional groups and organic chemistry grammar across C/P, B/B, and Behavioral Science when relevant.

Core terms:
Carboxylic acid, carboxylate, ester, ether, acyl, acetyl, methyl, amino group, amide, carbonyl, aldehyde, ketone, alcohol, hydroxyl, phosphate, sulfhydryl, anhydride, and related MCAT functional groups.

Goal:
Teach functional groups as the grammar of molecules. Neissa should learn how names and structures reveal reactivity, polarity, acid/base behavior, metabolism, macromolecule identity, and MCAT traps.

Do not include CARS. Do not turn this into a full organic reaction course. Focus on recognition, chemical logic, biological relevance, and common MCAT mistakes.

Use Kaplan and UWorld materials as the factual foundation.

Teaching style:
Speak like two calm MCAT tutors. Be slow, visual, and concrete. Teach by “functional group → chemical behavior → biological role.” Do not just list definitions.

Required sections:

1. Carbonyl family
Explain carbonyl as C=O. Connect aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, esters, amides, anhydrides, and acyl derivatives. Emphasize what changes around the carbonyl carbon.

2. Carboxylic acid family
Explain carboxylic acid vs carboxylate, acidity, resonance stabilization, amino acid side chains, fatty acids, and carboxylic acid derivatives.

3. Ester, ether, amide, and anhydride
Explain why these sound or look similar but behave differently. Clarify ester vs ether especially.

4. Acyl, acetyl, and methyl groups
Explain acyl as carbonyl-containing substituent, acetyl as a specific two-carbon acyl group, and methyl as CH3. Connect to acetyl-CoA, acetylcholine, methylation, and acyl transfer when relevant.

5. Nitrogen-containing groups
Explain amino group, amide, imine if relevant, and how nitrogen changes charge, basicity, hydrogen bonding, and biological function.

6. Oxygen, sulfur, and phosphate groups
Explain hydroxyl, alcohol, phenol, sulfhydryl/thiol, disulfide, phosphate, phosphodiester, and phosphorylation.

7. Reaction language
Explain hydrolysis, dehydration/condensation, oxidation, reduction, phosphorylation, dephosphorylation, methylation, acetylation, and decarboxylation as recurring modifications.

For each functional group or modification, include:
1. What it is in plain English
2. What structure to recognize
3. What behavior it creates: acidic, basic, polar, nonpolar, electrophilic, nucleophilic, hydrolyzable, oxidizable, reducible
4. Where it appears across C/P, B/B, and P/S when relevant
5. Common MCAT passage contexts
6. Common answer-choice traps
7. Similar-sounding or similar-looking terms
8. One reusable takeaway

Required comparisons:
- carbonyl vs carboxyl
- carboxylic acid vs carboxylate
- ester vs ether
- amide vs amine/amino group
- acyl vs acetyl vs methyl
- hydroxyl vs water vs alcohol
- phosphate vs phosphodiester vs phosphorylation
- hydrolysis vs dehydration/condensation
- oxidation vs reduction

End with:
1. Top recognition patterns
2. Top confusing pairs
3. Highest-yield MCAT traps
4. A 10-line cheat sheet Neissa can memorize
```
