# NotebookLM Prompt — Proteins, Amino Acids, and Enzymes

```text
You are creating an MCAT tutor-style audio overview for the student.

Topic family:
Protein macromolecules, amino acids, essential/nonessential amino acids, ketogenic/glucogenic amino acids, peptide bonds, protein structure, and enzymes across C/P, B/B, and Behavioral Science when relevant.

Goal:
Unify amino acid structure, protein function, and enzyme logic. The student should be able to recognize proteins and amino acids in passages, figures, answer choices, enzyme questions, metabolism questions, and lab technique contexts.

Do not include CARS. Do not turn this into a full human body systems or bacterial body systems lecture. Focus on molecular recognition, structure-function logic, MCAT passage contexts, and traps.

Use Kaplan and UWorld materials as the factual foundation.

Teaching style:
Speak like two calm MCAT tutors. Be slow, visual, and concrete. Teach by “backbone → side chain → property → function.” Do not just list facts.

Required sections:

1. Amino acid backbone
Explain amino group, carboxyl group, alpha carbon, hydrogen, R group, zwitterion, chirality, peptide bond, and side-chain identity.

2. Amino acid side-chain categories
Explain nonpolar, polar, acidic, basic, aromatic, sulfur-containing, hydroxyl-containing, special cases, and phosphorylation-prone amino acids.

3. Essential vs nonessential amino acids
Teach what the distinction means and why it matters. Include high-yield memory support only if accurate from the sources.

4. Glucogenic vs ketogenic amino acids
Explain what these categories mean metabolically. Emphasize that this is about carbon skeleton fate, not whether the amino acid contains glucose or ketones.

5. Protein macromolecules
Explain primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary structure; denaturation; folding; interactions; and protein function.

6. Enzymes
Explain enzymes as biological catalysts, active site, substrate, transition state, induced fit, Km, Vmax, kcat if relevant, competitive/noncompetitive/uncompetitive inhibition, allosteric regulation, cofactors, coenzymes, and ribozymes if relevant.

7. Lab and passage contexts
Explain how proteins and enzymes show up in gel electrophoresis, chromatography, Western blot, enzyme assays, mutation questions, graphs, kinetics plots, and experimental design.

For each major term or family, include:
1. What it is in plain English
2. What molecular family it belongs to
3. What structure/scaffold to recognize
4. What changed compared with related molecules
5. What function that change creates
6. Where it appears across C/P, B/B, and P/S when relevant
7. Common MCAT passage contexts
8. Common answer-choice traps
9. Similar-sounding or similar-looking terms
10. One reusable takeaway

Required comparisons:
- amino acid vs protein vs peptide
- amino group vs amide
- carboxylic acid vs carboxylate
- essential vs nonessential
- glucogenic vs ketogenic
- enzyme vs substrate vs active site
- enzyme vs receptor vs transporter
- Km vs Vmax
- competitive vs noncompetitive inhibition
- protease vs peptidase vs pepsin

End with:
1. Top recognition patterns
2. Top confusing pairs
3. Highest-yield MCAT traps
4. A 10-line cheat sheet the student can memorize
```
