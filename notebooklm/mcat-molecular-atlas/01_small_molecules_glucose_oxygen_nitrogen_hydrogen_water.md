# NotebookLM Prompt — Small Molecules: Glucose, Oxygen, Nitrogen, Hydrogen, Water

```text
You are creating an MCAT tutor-style audio overview for Neissa.

Topic family:
Glucose, oxygen, nitrogen, hydrogen, and water across C/P, B/B, and Behavioral Science when relevant.

Goal:
Teach these as recurring chemical characters, not isolated facts. Do not include CARS. Do not turn this into a full human body systems or bacterial body systems lecture. Focus on molecular recognition, chemical logic, function, MCAT passage contexts, and traps.

Use Kaplan and UWorld materials as the factual foundation.

Teaching style:
Speak like two calm MCAT tutors. Be slow, visual, and concrete. Teach by “scaffold → modification/context → function.” Do not just list facts. Explain how to recognize the molecule or term when it appears in a passage, answer choice, figure, graph, pathway, or explanation.

Required sections:

1. Glucose
Explain glucose as fuel, carbohydrate monomer, glycolysis input, glycogen building block, blood sugar signal, reducing sugar when relevant, and recurring structure in metabolism. Emphasize how glucose can appear as free glucose, glucose-6-phosphate, glycogen, or part of a larger carbohydrate.

2. Oxygen
Explain oxygen as molecular O2, final electron acceptor in aerobic respiration, gas exchange molecule, oxidation logic, partial pressure concept, and source of oxidative stress when relevant. Clarify oxygen atom vs oxygen gas vs oxygen in functional groups.

3. Nitrogen
Explain nitrogen as amino groups, amides, nucleotides, nitrogenous bases, amino acids, proteins, neurotransmitters, and urea cycle logic. Clarify nitrogen atom vs N2 gas vs nitrogen-containing biological molecules.

4. Hydrogen
Explain hydrogen as proton, pH, acid/base chemistry, hydrogen bonding, reduction/oxidation logic, electrochemical gradients, and membrane potentials when relevant. Clarify H atom vs H+ vs hydride-style reduction logic when appropriate.

5. Water
Explain water as solvent, acid/base participant, hydrolysis/condensation participant, osmosis solvent, heat capacity molecule, hydrogen-bonding system, and product/reactant in biochemical reactions.

For each molecule or concept, include:
1. What it is in plain English
2. What molecular family it belongs to

4. What role it plays across C/P, B/B, and P/S when relevant
5. Common MCAT passage contexts
6. Common answer-choice traps
7. Similar-looking or similar-sounding terms
8. One reusable takeaway

- Say “do not confuse X with Y” whenever useful.

- Connect structure to function.
- Point out when something is a molecule, ion, atom, solvent, gas, metabolite, functional group, or macromolecule component.

- Include short test-day recognition scripts.

End with:
1. Top recognition patterns
2. Top confusing pairs
```
