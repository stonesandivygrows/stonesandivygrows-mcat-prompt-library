# Reproduction → Fertilization → Embryogenesis → Neurulation Master Prompt

## Purpose
Create one continuous MCAT visual sequence that connects the menstrual cycle, oogenesis, fertilization, cleavage, implantation, gastrulation, germ layers, and neurulation without splitting ovarian, uterine, and embryonic events into disconnected sections.

## Source hierarchy
Use the student's Anki deck as the structural basis. Verify and fill gaps with Kaplan Biology Chapters 2 and 3, UWorld Biology, and Khan Academy. Keep detail at MCAT scope.

## Output requirements
- Use a left-to-right chronological pathway.
- Show ovarian events, uterine events, hormone levels, and embryonic events on aligned rows.
- Label every arrest point and every transition trigger.
- Distinguish follicle, oocyte, ovum, zygote, morula, and blastocyst.
- Add a correction box for common naming errors.

## Required sequence

### Menstruation and hormonal reset
- No fertilization/implantation → no hCG
- Corpus luteum degenerates into corpus albicans
- Progesterone and estrogen fall
- Functional endometrium breaks down and sheds
- Negative feedback on hypothalamus/anterior pituitary is removed
- Hypothalamus releases GnRH into the hypophyseal portal system
- Anterior pituitary releases FSH and LH

### Follicular phase
- FSH stimulates follicular growth
- Developing follicles produce estrogen
- Moderate estrogen gives negative feedback
- Sustained high estrogen switches to positive feedback
- Positive feedback produces the LH surge

### Oocyte maturation and ovulation
- Primary oocyte is arrested in prophase I
- LH surge causes completion of meiosis I
- Products: secondary oocyte + first polar body
- Secondary oocyte arrests in metaphase II
- LH surge triggers ovulation
- Mature follicle ruptures and releases the secondary oocyte
- Fimbriae sweep it into the uterine tube

### Luteal phase
- Ruptured follicle remains in ovary
- Ruptured follicle becomes corpus luteum
- Corpus luteum secretes progesterone and estrogen
- Estrogen establishes/proliferates endometrium
- Progesterone preserves/stabilizes secretory endometrium

### Fertilization branch
- Fertilization usually occurs in the ampulla
- Sperm entry triggers completion of meiosis II
- Products: ovum + second polar body
- Male and female pronuclei fuse → zygote
- Zygote undergoes cleavage without overall embryonic growth
- Blastomeres become progressively smaller
- Zygote → morula → blastocyst

### Blastocyst structure
- Trophoblast: outer layer, contributes to placenta and secretes hCG after implantation begins
- Inner cell mass/embryoblast: embryo proper
- Blastocoel: fluid-filled cavity
- Implantation occurs at the blastocyst stage

### Pregnancy branch
- Trophoblast-derived hCG maintains corpus luteum
- Corpus luteum continues progesterone and estrogen secretion
- Endometrium remains intact

### No-pregnancy branch
- No implantation → no hCG
- Corpus luteum degenerates
- Progesterone and estrogen fall
- Menstruation begins again

### Gastrulation
- Germ layers arise from the inner cell mass, not the trophoblast
- Ectoderm
- Mesoderm
- Endoderm
- Blastopore: opening formed during gastrulation
- Archenteron: primitive gut cavity

### Neurulation
- Notochord is mesoderm-derived
- Notochord induces overlying ectoderm
- Ectoderm → neural plate → neural groove/folds → neural tube
- Neural tube → brain and spinal cord
- Neural crest → peripheral nervous system, adrenal medulla, melanocytes, and selected craniofacial structures
- Surface ectoderm → epidermis and related structures

## Germ-layer derivative box

### Ectoderm
- Epidermis
- Nervous system
- Lens of eye
- Inner ear
- Adrenal medulla
- Lower anal canal

### Mesoderm
- Muscle
- Skeleton
- Connective tissue
- Circulatory system
- Gonads
- Adrenal cortex
- Most of the excretory system

### Endoderm
- Digestive tract epithelium
- Respiratory epithelium
- Pancreas
- Thyroid
- Bladder/distal urinary tract epithelium

## Required conceptual connections
- The structure sending a developmental signal does not have to become the induced structure.
- Mesoderm-derived notochord signals ectoderm to become nervous tissue.
- Trophoblast is support/placenta-related tissue; inner cell mass forms the embryo and germ layers.
- Blastocyst is the human implantation stage; blastula is the broader general embryology term.

## Common traps
- Corpus luteum does not revert to a follicle; it degenerates into corpus albicans.
- The follicle stays in the ovary; the secondary oocyte is released.
- Morula and blastocyst occur only after fertilization.
- The secondary oocyte, not a primary oocyte, is ovulated.
- hCG comes from trophoblast tissue after implantation begins.
- Blastocoel, blastopore, and archenteron are different structures.
