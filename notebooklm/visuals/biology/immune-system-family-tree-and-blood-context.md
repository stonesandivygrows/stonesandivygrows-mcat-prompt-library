## Blood composition

### Plasma
- Liquid portion of blood
- Contains water, albumin, clotting factors, electrolytes, nutrients, hormones, antibodies, and wastes
- Plasma is not a plasma cell
- In renal filtration, water and small solutes from plasma can enter Bowman’s space; cells and large proteins remain in blood

### Formed elements
- Red blood cells: oxygen transport
- Leukocytes/white blood cells: immune defense
- Platelets: megakaryocyte-derived cell fragments involved in hemostasis

## Leukocyte family tree

### Leukocytes = all white blood cells

#### Granulocytes
- Neutrophils
  - bacterial defense
  - acute inflammation
  - phagocytosis
  - chemotaxis and diapedesis/extravasation
- Eosinophils
  - parasites
  - allergy/asthma associations
- Basophils
  - histamine release
  - allergic inflammation

#### Agranulocytes
- Monocytes
  - can differentiate into macrophages and dendritic cells
- Lymphocytes
  - B cells
  - T cells
  - natural killer cells

## Classification warning
- Granulocyte/agranulocyte describes cell appearance.
- Phagocyte describes function.
- Therefore, a neutrophil can be both a granulocyte and a phagocyte.

## Innate immunity

### Barriers and molecules
- Skin
- Mucus
- Respiratory cilia
- Lysozyme in tears and saliva
- Gastric acid, not gastrin, kills many swallowed pathogens
- Defensins disrupt microbial membranes
- Complement
- Interferons
- Normal flora

### Cells
- Neutrophils
- Macrophages
- Dendritic cells
- Natural killer cells
- Eosinophils
- Basophils/mast cells

### Required distinctions
- Lysozyme: antibacterial enzyme in secretions
- Lysosome: acidic intracellular digestive organelle
- Lysosomal enzymes function best at low pH, not high pH
- Peroxisomes use oxidative enzymes such as catalase; do not confuse them with lysosomes

### Pattern recognition
Explain that innate cells recognize conserved pathogen-associated molecular patterns rather than unique antigen specificity. Examples may include bacterial flagellin or unusual microbial nucleic-acid patterns, but do not overextend beyond MCAT scope.

### Dendritic cells
- Capture antigen in tissues
- Present antigen to T cells
- Major bridge between innate and adaptive immunity

### Natural killer cells
- Innate lymphocytes
- Kill virally infected and cancerous cells, especially cells with reduced/abnormal MHC I expression

## Adaptive immunity

### Humoral immunity
- B-cell and antibody mediated
- Naive B cell recognizes antigen and receives activation signals
- Clonal expansion
- Plasma cells secrete antibodies
- Memory B cells remain for a faster future response

### Antibody functions
- Neutralization
- Opsonization
- Agglutination
- Complement activation

### Cell-mediated immunity
- T-cell mediated

#### Helper T cells
- CD4+
- Recognize antigen on MHC II
- Release cytokines and coordinate B cells, macrophages, and cytotoxic T cells

#### Cytotoxic T cells
- CD8+
- Recognize abnormal intracellular antigen displayed on MHC I
- Kill infected or cancerous host cells

#### Regulatory T cells
- Suppress excessive immune responses
- Limit tissue damage
- Help prevent autoimmunity
- Memory phrase: “Everybody pack it up.”

#### Memory T cells
- Persist after initial exposure
- Produce a faster, stronger response upon re-exposure

## MHC comparison

### MHC I
- Found on almost all nucleated cells
- Presents endogenous/intracellular antigen
- Recognized by CD8+ cytotoxic T cells
- Cytotoxic T cells kill the displaying cell, not the MHC molecule

### MHC II
- Found on professional antigen-presenting cells
- Dendritic cells, macrophages, and B cells
- Presents exogenous/extracellular antigen
- Recognized by CD4+ helper T cells

## Inflammation and diapedesis
- Vasodilation increases local blood flow
- Vascular permeability increases
- Endothelium expresses adhesion molecules
- Leukocytes roll, adhere, and pass between endothelial cells
- Diapedesis/extravasation = leukocyte exit from bloodstream into tissue
- Connect glycosylated surface molecules with cell recognition without requiring excessive molecular detail

## High-confusion terms
- Plasma vs plasma cell
- Leukocyte vs lymphocyte
- Monocyte vs macrophage
- Granulocyte vs phagocyte
- Lysozyme vs lysosome
- Gastrin vs gastric acid
- Innate lymphocyte NK cell vs adaptive B/T lymphocytes
- Antibody on B-cell surface vs secreted antibody from plasma cells
