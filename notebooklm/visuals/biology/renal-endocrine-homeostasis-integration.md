# Renal–Endocrine Homeostasis Integration Prompt

## Purpose
Create a single, visually organized MCAT review that integrates nephron physiology with endocrine control of osmolarity, blood volume, blood pressure, and electrolyte balance.

## Source hierarchy
Use the student's Anki deck as the structural basis. Verify and fill gaps with Kaplan Biology Chapters 5, 7, and 10, UWorld Biology, and Khan Academy. Do not add clinical detail beyond MCAT scope unless needed to resolve a contradiction.

## Output requirements
- Build one continuous nephron pathway from glomerulus to collecting duct.
- Keep directionality explicit with arrows.
- Distinguish blood, interstitial fluid, tubular epithelial cell, and tubule lumen.
- Flag common traps and incorrect shortcuts.
- Use concise MCAT-level explanations.

## Required content

### Core movement vocabulary
- Filtration: blood in glomerular capillaries → Bowman’s space/filtrate
- Reabsorption: tubule lumen → epithelial cell → interstitium → peritubular capillary or vasa recta
- Secretion: peritubular capillary → interstitium → epithelial cell → tubule lumen
- Excretion: tubule/collecting system → outside body

### Filtration barrier
Explain that glomerular filtration is mainly pressure-, size-, and charge-dependent rather than the same as passive diffusion across a lipid bilayer.
- Filtered: water, ions, glucose, amino acids, urea, small metabolites
- Retained: blood cells, platelets, large plasma proteins such as albumin

### Nephron sequence
1. Glomerulus/Bowman’s capsule
   - filtration only
2. Proximal convoluted tubule
   - bulk reabsorption of glucose, amino acids, vitamins, NaCl, and water
   - secretion of H+, K+, NH3/NH4+, urea, and drug metabolites as appropriate
3. Descending loop of Henle
   - permeable to water
   - relatively impermeable to salt
   - water leaves because medullary interstitium is hyperosmotic
4. Ascending loop of Henle
   - reabsorbs salt
   - impermeable to water
   - emphasize: water follows salt only when the membrane is permeable to water
5. Distal convoluted tubule
   - fine-tuning of ions
   - aldosterone-sensitive region
   - secretion of K+ and H+
6. Collecting duct
   - final control of water balance
   - ADH-dependent aquaporin insertion

### Medullary gradient
Use this exact relationship:
- Salt reabsorption establishes the hyperosmotic medulla.
- The medullary gradient provides the osmotic pull.
- ADH opens the water pathway by inserting aquaporins.

### Hormone comparison

#### ADH
- Trigger: high plasma osmolarity and/or sufficiently low blood volume/pressure
- Source: synthesized in hypothalamus, released by posterior pituitary
- Target: mainly collecting duct; late DCT may be included if source uses it
- Mechanism: inserts preformed aquaporins
- Effect: water reabsorption increases, plasma osmolarity decreases, blood volume increases, urine becomes concentrated

#### RAAS and aldosterone
- Low renal perfusion, low NaCl delivery, or sympathetic β1 stimulation → renin
- Renin → angiotensin I → ACE → angiotensin II
- Angiotensin II → vasoconstriction + aldosterone release
- Aldosterone: reabsorbs Na+, secretes K+ and H+; water follows Na+ when permeability allows
- Main result: increased ECF volume and blood pressure with little net change in plasma osmolarity

#### ANP
- Trigger: high blood volume/atrial stretch
- Promotes Na+ and water excretion
- Opposes RAAS

### Integrated stimulant example
Use one MCAT-level example with amphetamine/Adderall:
- Sympathetic activation can increase heart rate and blood pressure.
- Mild dehydration mainly raises osmolarity → ADH is the cleaner answer.
- Significant volume loss or reduced renal perfusion can also activate RAAS.
- Do not frame the body as consciously choosing only one hormone.

## Required distinction box
- Concentration problem → ADH
- Volume/pressure problem → RAAS/aldosterone
- Excess volume/pressure → ANP

## Common traps
- ADH does not mainly create new aquaporins in the acute MCAT mechanism; it inserts existing ones.
- Descending limb: water out.
- Ascending limb: salt out, water stays.
- Filtration occurs at Bowman’s capsule; secretion occurs later along the nephron.
- ADH dilutes blood while concentrating urine.
- Aldosterone is not simply “the blood pressure hormone”; it also responds to high K+.
