## Poster 1 — Waves Big Picture: Repeating Disturbances Carry Energy

Audio purpose: orient the listener to waves as energy transfer without bulk matter transfer.

Main visual: transverse wave and longitudinal wave side by side.

Required formulas:
- v = fλ
- T = 1/f

Visual elements:
- Amplitude
- Wavelength
- Frequency
- Period
- Wave speed
- Transverse vs longitudinal comparison

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking waves move matter across the whole distance. Fix: waves transfer energy; particles oscillate locally.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces waves, wavelength, frequency, period, amplitude, or wave speed.

## Poster 2 — Wave Equation and Graph Reading

Audio purpose: make wave graphs and variable relationships automatic.

Main visual: labeled wave graph with amplitude and wavelength plus equation dashboard.

Required formulas:
- v = fλ
- f = 1/T

Visual elements:
- Crest/trough labels
- Amplitude vs wavelength
- Frequency vs period inverse relationship
- Same medium wave speed rule

MCAT trap strip: Trap: confusing amplitude with wavelength or frequency. Fix: amplitude is height; wavelength is horizontal cycle length; frequency is cycles per second.

## Poster 4 — Intensity and Decibels

Audio purpose: explain sound level and logarithmic scaling.

Main visual: speaker radiating sound spheres outward.

Required formulas:
- I = P/A
- β = 10 log(I/I0)

Visual elements:
- Intensity decreases with distance
- Decibel scale as logarithmic
- 10× intensity increase = +10 dB
- 100× intensity increase = +20 dB

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating decibels as linear. Fix: decibels are logarithmic, so small dB changes can mean large intensity ratios.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says intensity, decibel, loudness, logarithm, or sound level.

## Poster 5 — Doppler Effect

Audio purpose: visually show frequency shifts from source/observer motion.

Main visual: ambulance moving toward and away from observer with compressed and stretched wavefronts.

Required relationship:
- Moving closer → observed frequency increases
- Moving apart → observed frequency decreases

Visual elements:
- Wavefront bunching in front of source
- Wavefront spreading behind source
- Source moving vs observer moving comparison
- Medical ultrasound bridge if audio mentions it

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking the emitted frequency changes. Fix: the observed frequency changes because relative motion changes wavefront arrival rate.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says Doppler, moving source, moving observer, higher pitch, or lower pitch.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch07_Audio_05_Doppler_Effect.png`
