## Poster 1 — Fluids Big Picture: Pressure, Flow, Buoyancy

Audio purpose: orient the listener to the chapter as “fluids respond to pressure gradients and displaced volume.”

Main visual: connected water tank, floating object, narrowing pipe, and blood vessel bridge.

Required formulas:
- ρ = m/V
- P = F/A
- ΔP drives flow

Visual elements:
- Static fluid vs flowing fluid split
- Pressure arrows
- Flow arrows from high pressure to low pressure
- Floating/sinking preview

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating fluid pressure as only “downward.” Fix: pressure in a fluid acts in all directions at a point.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces density, pressure, buoyancy, flow, or circulatory/respiratory applications.

## Poster 2 — Density, Specific Gravity, Hydrostatic Pressure

Audio purpose: make density and depth-based pressure automatic.

Main visual: vertical fluid column with pressure increasing with depth.

Required formulas:
- ρ = m/V
- SG = ρsubstance/ρwater
- P = P0 + ρgh

Visual elements:
- Deeper point has greater pressure
- Same depth = same pressure in connected fluid
- Density comparison blocks
- Specific gravity quick interpretation

MCAT trap strip: Trap: pressure depends on container shape. Fix: hydrostatic pressure depends on depth, density, and gravity, not container shape.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says density, specific gravity, depth, hydrostatic pressure, or connected vessels.

## Poster 3 — Buoyancy and Archimedes’ Principle

Audio purpose: show floating/sinking as displaced-fluid logic.

Main visual: object partly submerged with upward buoyant force and downward weight.

Required formulas:
- Fb = ρfluid Vdisplaced g
- Weight = mg

Visual elements:
- Floating: Fb = weight
- Sinking: weight > Fb
- Rising: Fb > weight
- More displaced volume increases buoyant force

MCAT trap strip: Trap: buoyant force depends on the object’s density directly. Fix: buoyant force depends on fluid density and displaced volume; object density determines floating behavior by comparison.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says buoyancy, displaced fluid, floating, sinking, or Archimedes.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch04_Audio_03_Buoyancy_Archimedes.png`

## Poster 4 — Pascal’s Law and Hydraulic Systems

Audio purpose: connect pressure transmission to force amplification.

Main visual: hydraulic lift with small piston and large piston.

Required formulas:
- P = F/A
- P1 = P2
- F1/A1 = F2/A2

Visual elements:
- Same pressure transmitted through enclosed fluid
- Larger area produces larger force
- Distance tradeoff to conserve work

MCAT trap strip: Trap: hydraulic systems create energy. Fix: they amplify force by trading distance; work is conserved ideally.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says Pascal, hydraulic lift, pressure transmission, or force amplification.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch04_Audio_04_Pascal_Hydraulics.png`

## Poster 5 — Continuity Equation: Flow Rate and Tube Area

Audio purpose: make narrowing/widening tube logic visual.

Main visual: pipe narrowing with streamlines speeding up.

Required formulas:
- Q = Av
- A1v1 = A2v2 for incompressible flow

Visual elements:
- Smaller area → higher speed
- Larger area → lower speed
- Flow rate conserved through a closed tube
- Blood-vessel analogy

MCAT trap strip: Trap: confusing speed with flow rate. Fix: speed changes with area, but volume flow rate is conserved in steady incompressible flow.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says continuity, flow rate, narrowing vessel, or cross-sectional area.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch04_Audio_05_Continuity_Flow_Rate.png`

## Poster 6 — Bernoulli’s Equation: Pressure, Speed, Height Tradeoffs

Audio purpose: explain energy conservation in moving fluids.

Main visual: horizontal pipe narrowing and vertical pipe with height change.

Required formulas:
- P + 1/2ρv² + ρgh = constant

Visual elements:
- Faster flow often means lower static pressure
- Higher elevation trades pressure/kinetic energy
- Venturi effect diagram
- Blood vessel stenosis bridge

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming faster fluid always means higher pressure. Fix: in Bernoulli ideal flow, higher speed often corresponds to lower static pressure.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says Bernoulli, Venturi, pressure-speed tradeoff, or stenosis.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch04_Audio_06_Bernoulli_Pressure_Speed.png`

## Poster 7 — Viscosity and Poiseuille’s Law

Audio purpose: make resistance to fluid flow especially radius dependence unforgettable.

Main visual: blood vessel with radius changing and flow changing dramatically.

Required formulas:
- Q = πr⁴ΔP/(8ηL)
- Resistance ∝ ηL/r⁴

Visual elements:
- Radius fourth-power warning
- Higher viscosity decreases flow
- Longer tube decreases flow
- Larger pressure gradient increases flow

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating vessel radius as a small detail. Fix: radius has a fourth-power effect, so small radius changes massively alter flow.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says viscosity, Poiseuille, radius, vessel resistance, or blood flow.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch04_Audio_07_Viscosity_Poiseuille.png`

## Poster 8 — Surface Tension, Capillary Action, Respiratory Bridge

Audio purpose: connect fluid surfaces to lungs and small tubes.

Main visual: water droplet, capillary tube, and alveolus with surfactant.

Required concepts:
- Cohesion vs adhesion
- Surface tension resists surface expansion
- Capillary rise depends on tube radius and fluid-wall interactions

Visual elements:
- Meniscus shapes
- Small capillary rise
- Alveolar surface tension and surfactant
- Lung collapse prevention bridge

MCAT trap strip: Trap: surfactant increases surface tension. Fix: surfactant decreases alveolar surface tension and helps keep alveoli open.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says surface tension, capillary action, cohesion, adhesion, surfactant, or alveoli.
