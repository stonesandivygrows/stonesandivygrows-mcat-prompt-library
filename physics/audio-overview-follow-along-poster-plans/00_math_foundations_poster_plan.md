# Physics 0 — Math Foundations Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 7 posters

Audio journey: start with unit discipline, then scientific notation, vectors/trig, proportional reasoning, graphs, and error-proof arithmetic.

## Poster 1 — Math Big Picture: The MCAT Physics Toolkit

Audio purpose: orient the listener to math as a shortcut system, not a separate subject.

Main visual: toolbox with unit conversion, vectors, trig, logs, graphs, and estimation tools.

Required formulas: dimensional analysis setup, slope = Δy/Δx, area under graph.

Visual elements:
- Tool icons for units, powers of ten, trig, graphs, and proportions
- Left-to-right problem-solving workflow
- “Math exists to avoid panic” reminder

MCAT trap strip: Trap: doing algebra before checking units. Fix: units tell you what equation shape is possible.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces the math chapter or says “before physics, know the tools.”

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_01_Math_Toolkit_Overview.png`

## Poster 2 — Units and Dimensional Analysis

Audio purpose: make units the first-pass strategy for C/P calculations.

Main visual: unit-conversion railroad track.

Required formulas: unit cancellation layout; SI base units for m, kg, s, A, K, mol.

Visual elements:
- Numerator/denominator cancellation lanes
- Common prefixes: pico, nano, micro, milli, centi, kilo, mega
- Unit sanity check box

MCAT trap strip: Trap: memorizing equations but ignoring whether units match. Fix: final units should match the answer choice type.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says conversion, SI units, prefixes, or dimensional analysis.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_02_Units_Dimensional_Analysis.png`

## Poster 3 — Scientific Notation and Estimation

Audio purpose: reduce arithmetic fatigue and prevent exponent slips.

Main visual: powers-of-ten ladder.

Required formulas: scientific notation normalization; multiplication/division exponent rules.

Visual elements:
- 10⁻¹² to 10⁶ prefix ladder
- Moving decimal left/right examples
- Estimate-before-calculating workflow

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating 10⁻³ and 10³ like small formatting details. Fix: powers of ten usually decide the answer.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio discusses rough math, prefixes, or answer-choice elimination.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_03_Scientific_Notation_Estimation.png`

## Poster 4 — Vectors, Components, Dot Product, Cross Product

Audio purpose: connect vector math to forces, work, torque, and magnetism.

Main visual: vector broken into x/y components plus dot vs cross comparison.

Required formulas: Ax = A cosθ, Ay = A sinθ, dot = AB cosθ, cross magnitude = AB sinθ.

Visual elements:
- Component triangle
- Dot product = alignment meter
- Cross product = perpendicular/right-hand rule meter
- MCAT uses: work, torque, magnetic force

MCAT trap strip: Trap: using sine/cosine without asking what side is adjacent or opposite. Fix: draw the triangle first.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says vector, component, angle, work, torque, or right-hand rule.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_04_Vectors_Dot_Cross_Products.png`

## Poster 5 — Ratios, Proportions, and Scaling

Audio purpose: teach quick MCAT proportional reasoning without full algebra.

Main visual: equation balance scale showing direct, inverse, square, and inverse-square relationships.

Required formulas: y ∝ x, y ∝ 1/x, y ∝ x², y ∝ 1/x².

Visual elements:
- “If x doubles” comparison table
- Radius-power warning box
- Examples: Coulomb, gravity, Poiseuille, intensity

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating radius, distance, and area as linear when the equation squares or fourth-powers them. Fix: identify the exponent first.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio asks “what happens if this doubles?”

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_05_Ratios_Proportions_Scaling.png`

## Poster 6 — Graph Reading: Slope, Area, Shape

Audio purpose: make graphs active evidence instead of visual noise.

Main visual: three graph panels: slope, area, curve shape.

Required formulas: slope = Δy/Δx; area under curve = accumulated quantity.

Visual elements:
- Position-time slope gives velocity
- Velocity-time slope gives acceleration
- Velocity-time area gives displacement
- Force-distance area gives work

MCAT trap strip: Trap: reading graph height when the question asks for slope or area. Fix: identify what graph operation is being tested.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says graph, slope, area, trend, or relationship.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_06_Graph_Reading_Slope_Area.png`

## Poster 7 — Math Traps and Whiteboard Rescue

Audio purpose: create a test-day error checklist.

Main visual: “before calculating” checkpoint board.

Required formulas: none; this is a strategy poster.

Visual elements:
- Convert units
- Check Kelvin vs Celsius
- Isolate variables before plugging numbers
- Estimate answer magnitude
- Watch 6/7/8 arithmetic facts

MCAT trap strip: Trap: arithmetic overconfidence under time pressure. Fix: estimate first, calculate second, check units last.

Audio-follow cue: use at the end of math overview or before practice sets.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch00_Audio_07_Math_Traps_Whiteboard_Rescue.png`
