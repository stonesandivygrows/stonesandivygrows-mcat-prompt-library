# Physics 2 — Work and Energy Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 8 posters

Audio journey: define work as force along displacement, then connect kinetic energy, potential energy, power, machines, spring energy, stress/strain, and MCAT traps.

## Poster 1 — Energy Big Picture: Transfer, Storage, Conservation

Audio purpose: orient the listener to energy as a bookkeeping system.

Main visual: energy flow map showing work transferring energy into kinetic, gravitational, spring, thermal, and mechanical forms.

Required formulas:
- W = ΔE
- ΔEtotal = 0 for conserved systems

Visual elements:
- Energy transfer arrows
- Conservative vs nonconservative split
- System boundary reminder

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating energy as a force. Fix: energy is capacity/bookkeeping; work is energy transfer by force through displacement.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces work, energy, conservation, or “where did the energy go?”

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_01_Energy_Big_Picture.png`

## Poster 2 — Work: Force Along Displacement

Audio purpose: make work a dot-product story.

Main visual: object pulled at an angle with force vector split into parallel and perpendicular components.

Required formulas:
- W = Fd cosθ
- Wnet = ΔKE

Visual elements:
- θ = 0° gives maximum positive work
- θ = 90° gives zero work
- θ = 180° gives negative work
- Perpendicular force does no work

MCAT trap strip: Trap: any force means work. Fix: only the force component along displacement does work.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says work, angle, dot product, parallel force, or perpendicular force.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_02_Work_Dot_Product.png`

## Poster 3 — Kinetic and Potential Energy

Audio purpose: show stored and moving energy visually.

Main visual: roller coaster with high point, low point, spring, and moving cart.

Required formulas:
- KE = 1/2mv²
- PEg = mgh
- PEspring = 1/2kx²

Visual elements:
- Height converts to speed
- Spring compression stores energy
- Velocity squared warning

MCAT trap strip: Trap: doubling velocity doubles KE. Fix: kinetic energy depends on v², so doubling v quadruples KE.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says kinetic energy, potential energy, spring energy, height, or speed.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_03_KE_PE_Spring_Energy.png`

## Poster 4 — Conservation of Mechanical Energy

Audio purpose: help the listener track energy swaps without overcalculating.

Main visual: before/after energy bar charts for a falling object and spring launch.

Required formulas:
- KEi + PEi = KEf + PEf when no nonconservative work
- Wnonconservative = ΔEmechanical

Visual elements:
- Mechanical energy bar stacks
- Friction drains mechanical energy to thermal energy
- Initial/final setup boxes

MCAT trap strip: Trap: applying mechanical energy conservation when friction does work. Fix: include nonconservative work or energy loss.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says conservation, frictionless, final speed, or energy transformation.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_04_Conservation_Mechanical_Energy.png`

## Poster 5 — Power and Rate of Energy Transfer

Audio purpose: distinguish total work from how fast work is done.

Main visual: two machines lifting the same mass to the same height at different speeds.

Required formulas:
- P = W/t
- P = ΔE/t
- P = Fv when force and velocity align

Visual elements:
- Same work, different power
- Watts as J/s
- Faster energy transfer = higher power

MCAT trap strip: Trap: more power means more total work. Fix: power is rate; same work can be done at different power levels.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says power, watts, rate, lifting faster, or energy per time.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_05_Power_Energy_Rate.png`

## Poster 6 — Simple Machines and Mechanical Advantage

Audio purpose: explain machines as force-distance tradeoffs, not energy creators.

Main visual: lever, pulley, inclined plane, and hydraulic/gear analogy if mentioned.

Required formulas:
- Mechanical advantage = output force / input force
- Work in ≈ work out for ideal machine

Visual elements:
- Input force over long distance
- Output force over short distance
- Energy conservation box

MCAT trap strip: Trap: simple machines reduce work. Fix: ideal machines reduce force by increasing distance; work is conserved.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says lever, pulley, ramp, mechanical advantage, or simple machine.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_06_Simple_Machines_Mechanical_Advantage.png`

## Poster 7 — Stress, Strain, Elasticity

Audio purpose: connect work/energy to materials and biological tissues.

Main visual: material sample being stretched with force arrows.

Required formulas:
- Stress = F/A
- Strain = ΔL/L
- Young’s modulus = stress/strain

Visual elements:
- Elastic region vs plastic deformation
- Stiff vs flexible material comparison
- Biological bridge: tendons, bone, vessels

MCAT trap strip: Trap: stress and strain are the same. Fix: stress is applied force per area; strain is fractional deformation.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says elasticity, deformation, Young’s modulus, stress, or strain.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_07_Stress_Strain_Elasticity.png`

## Poster 8 — Work and Energy MCAT Trap Board

Audio purpose: final rescue poster for this chapter.

Main visual: decision board: work-energy theorem, conservation, power, and stress/strain.

Required formulas: W = Fd cosθ, KE = 1/2mv², PE = mgh, P = W/t, stress = F/A.

Visual elements:
- Work sign chart
- Conservative vs nonconservative table
- Energy vs momentum distinction note
- Units checklist

MCAT trap strip: Trap: using energy when momentum is the conserved quantity, or using momentum when energy is easier. Fix: collisions often need momentum; height/speed/frictionless systems often need energy.

Audio-follow cue: use at the end of the audio and before practice sets.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch02_Audio_08_Work_Energy_MCAT_Traps.png`
