# Physics 3 — Thermodynamics Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 9 posters

Audio journey: define systems and heat, then move through calorimetry, phase changes, the first law, PV work, thermodynamic processes, entropy, heat engines, and traps.

## Poster 1 — Thermodynamics Big Picture: System, Surroundings, Energy Accounting

Audio purpose: orient the listener to thermodynamics as boundary-based energy bookkeeping.

Main visual: system boundary around gas in piston, with surroundings outside.

Required formulas:
- ΔU = Q − W when W is work done by system
- ΔU = Q + W when W is work done on system

Visual elements:
- System vs surroundings
- Heat arrows
- Work arrows
- Internal energy storage
- Sign convention warning

MCAT trap strip: Trap: memorizing one first-law sign convention without checking definition of W. Fix: read whether work is done by or on the system.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says system, surroundings, internal energy, heat, work, or first law.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_01_Thermo_Big_Picture.png`

## Poster 2 — Temperature, Heat, and Calorimetry

Audio purpose: separate temperature from heat transfer.

Main visual: hot object and cold water in a calorimeter reaching equilibrium.

Required formulas:
- q = mcΔT
- qlost + qgained = 0 for isolated calorimetry

Visual elements:
- Temperature = average molecular kinetic energy
- Heat = energy transfer due to temperature difference
- Calorimeter as insulated energy box

MCAT trap strip: Trap: heat and temperature are identical. Fix: temperature is a state measurement; heat is energy in transit.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says calorimetry, specific heat, heat transfer, or thermal equilibrium.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_02_Heat_Temperature_Calorimetry.png`

## Poster 3 — Phase Changes and Heating Curves

Audio purpose: show when energy changes temperature versus phase.

Main visual: heating curve with sloped segments and plateaus.

Required formulas:
- q = mcΔT for temperature changes
- q = mL for phase changes

Visual elements:
- Solid, liquid, gas regions
- Melting and boiling plateaus
- Sensible heat vs latent heat
- Temperature constant during phase change

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming added heat always raises temperature. Fix: during phase change, heat changes intermolecular spacing/phase, not temperature.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says latent heat, fusion, vaporization, phase change, or heating curve.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_03_Phase_Changes_Heating_Curve.png`

## Poster 4 — First Law and State Functions

Audio purpose: anchor the conservation logic of thermodynamics.

Main visual: accounting ledger with ΔU, Q, and W columns.

Required formulas:
- ΔU = Q − W_by_system
- ΔU depends only on state, not path

Visual elements:
- State functions: pressure, volume, temperature, internal energy
- Path functions: heat and work
- Closed loop returns state functions to zero net change

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating heat and work as stored inside a system. Fix: heat and work are transfer processes, not state functions.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says first law, state function, path function, or internal energy.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_04_First_Law_State_Functions.png`

## Poster 5 — PV Work and Gas Expansion

Audio purpose: make expansion/compression work visual.

Main visual: piston expanding and compressing gas.

Required formulas:
- W = PΔV for constant external pressure
- Area under P-V curve = work

Visual elements:
- Expansion: gas pushes piston outward
- Compression: surroundings push piston inward
- P-V graph area shading
- Sign convention note

MCAT trap strip: Trap: using PΔV without tracking expansion versus compression. Fix: determine whether the gas did work or had work done on it.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says piston, expansion, compression, pressure-volume work, or P-V graph.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_05_PV_Work_Gas_Expansion.png`

## Poster 6 — Thermodynamic Processes

Audio purpose: compare isothermal, adiabatic, isobaric, and isovolumetric processes.

Main visual: four-process comparison grid with tiny P-V curves.

Required relationships:
- Isothermal: ΔT = 0
- Adiabatic: Q = 0
- Isobaric: ΔP = 0
- Isovolumetric: ΔV = 0 and W = 0

Visual elements:
- Process name breakdown
- What stays constant
- What equation simplification follows
- P-V curve shape hints

MCAT trap strip: Trap: mixing up isothermal and adiabatic. Fix: isothermal keeps temperature constant; adiabatic has no heat exchange.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says thermodynamic process, isothermal, adiabatic, isobaric, or isovolumetric.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_06_Thermodynamic_Processes.png`

## Poster 7 — Entropy and Energy Dispersal

Audio purpose: explain entropy without relying on vague “messiness.”

Main visual: energy packets spreading into more possible microstates.

Required formulas:
- ΔS = Qrev/T conceptually

Visual elements:
- More microstates = higher entropy
- Solid → liquid → gas entropy trend
- Increased volume increases entropy
- Energy dispersal visual

MCAT trap strip: Trap: entropy equals chaos only. Fix: better frame is energy dispersal and number of accessible microstates.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says entropy, disorder, microstates, energy dispersal, or second law.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_07_Entropy_Energy_Dispersal.png`

## Poster 8 — Heat Engines, Efficiency, and Second Law

Audio purpose: connect heat flow to useful work and limits.

Main visual: heat engine between hot reservoir and cold reservoir.

Required formulas:
- efficiency = Wout/Qhot
- Carnot efficiency = 1 − Tcold/Thot

Visual elements:
- Hot reservoir input
- Work output
- Waste heat to cold reservoir
- Why 100% efficiency is impossible

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming all heat can become work. Fix: some heat must be rejected to a colder reservoir.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says heat engine, efficiency, Carnot, reservoir, or second law.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch03_Audio_08_Heat_Engines_Efficiency.png`
