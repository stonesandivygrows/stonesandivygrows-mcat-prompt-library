# Physics 9 — Atomic and Nuclear Phenomena Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 10 posters

Audio journey: start with quantization and photons, then atomic transitions, photoelectric effect, de Broglie logic, radioactive decay, half-life, mass defect, binding energy, fission/fusion, and medical/radiation traps.

## Poster 1 — Atomic and Nuclear Big Picture: Quantized Energy and Unstable Nuclei

Audio purpose: orient the listener to the chapter split: electron-level energy vs nucleus-level energy.

Main visual: atom diagram split into electron shell region and nucleus region.

Required formulas:
- E = hf
- E = hc/λ
- E = mc²

Visual elements:
- Electron transitions use photon energy
- Nuclei use binding energy and mass defect
- Atomic scale vs nuclear scale comparison
- Radiation types preview

MCAT trap strip: Trap: blending atomic electron transitions with nuclear decay. Fix: atomic phenomena involve electrons/photons; nuclear phenomena involve protons, neutrons, and nuclear stability.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces atomic phenomena, nuclear phenomena, photons, energy levels, or radioactivity.

## Poster 2 — Photons and Quantized Energy

Audio purpose: make photon energy relationships automatic.

Main visual: photon packets with different wavelengths and energy labels.

Required formulas:
- E = hf
- c = fλ
- E = hc/λ

Visual elements:
- Higher frequency = higher energy
- Shorter wavelength = higher energy
- Photon energy is quantized
- Visible/UV/X-ray comparison

MCAT trap strip: Trap: increasing light intensity increases photon energy. Fix: photon energy depends on frequency, not intensity.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says photon, quantized, frequency, wavelength, Planck, or energy packet.

## Poster 3 — Bohr Model and Electron Transitions

Audio purpose: visualize absorption and emission as jumps between energy levels.

Main visual: electron energy ladder with upward and downward arrows.

Required concepts:
- Absorption: electron moves up by taking in photon
- Emission: electron drops down and releases photon
- Photon energy equals gap size

Visual elements:
- Energy levels not continuous
- Larger gap = higher energy photon
- Emission spectrum lines
- Absorption spectrum missing lines

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking electrons emit light when they jump up. Fix: absorption moves electron up; emission occurs when electron falls down.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says Bohr model, electron transition, absorption, emission, spectral line, or energy level.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch09_Audio_03_Bohr_Electron_Transitions.png`

## Poster 4 — Photoelectric Effect

Audio purpose: clarify threshold frequency, intensity, and electron kinetic energy.

Main visual: light hitting metal surface and ejecting electrons.

Required formulas:
- KEmax = hf − φ
- threshold frequency: hf0 = φ

Visual elements:
- Frequency below threshold: no electrons ejected
- Frequency above threshold: electrons ejected
- Higher frequency → higher electron KE
- Higher intensity above threshold → more electrons, not more KE per electron

MCAT trap strip: Trap: brighter low-frequency light ejects electrons. Fix: no electrons eject unless individual photons meet threshold frequency.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says photoelectric effect, work function, threshold frequency, stopping potential, or intensity.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch09_Audio_04_Photoelectric_Effect.png`

## Poster 5 — de Broglie and Wave-Particle Duality

Audio purpose: show why particles can behave like waves.

Main visual: electron with wave pattern and momentum arrow.

Required formulas:
- λ = h/p
- p = mv for nonrelativistic particles

Visual elements:
- Higher momentum → shorter wavelength
- Small particles show wave behavior more obviously
- Electron diffraction bridge
- Wave-particle duality comparison

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming only light has wave-particle duality. Fix: matter also has de Broglie wavelength, but it is usually tiny for large objects.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says de Broglie, matter wave, momentum, wavelength, or wave-particle duality.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch09_Audio_05_DeBroglie_Wave_Particle_Duality.png`

## Poster 6 — Radioactive Decay Types

Audio purpose: make nuclear bookkeeping visual.

Main visual: nucleus undergoing alpha, beta minus, beta plus, electron capture, and gamma emission.

Required decay rules:
- Alpha: A − 4, Z − 2
- Beta minus: Z + 1
- Beta plus: Z − 1
- Electron capture: Z − 1
- Gamma: no A or Z change

Visual elements:
- Parent nucleus → daughter nucleus arrows
- Atomic number and mass number changes
- Particle identity icons
- Charge conservation note

MCAT trap strip: Trap: changing mass number during beta decay. Fix: beta decay changes atomic number, not mass number.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says alpha, beta, gamma, positron, electron capture, parent isotope, or daughter isotope.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch09_Audio_06_Radioactive_Decay_Types.png`

## Poster 7 — Half-Life and Exponential Decay

Audio purpose: reduce decay arithmetic friction.

Main visual: radioactive sample halving over time with graph.

Required formulas:
- N = N0(1/2)^(t/t1/2)
- Activity conceptually decreases with number of undecayed nuclei

Visual elements:
- 100% → 50% → 25% → 12.5%
- Half-life step table
- Exponential curve
- Time elapsed divided by half-life

MCAT trap strip: Trap: subtracting a constant amount each half-life. Fix: half-life removes a fraction, not a fixed absolute amount.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says half-life, decay curve, remaining sample, activity, or isotope dating.

## Poster 8 — Mass Defect and Binding Energy

Audio purpose: make nuclear binding energy conceptually clear.

Main visual: separate nucleons weighing more than bound nucleus, with missing mass converted to binding energy.

Required formulas:
- Δm = mass of separated nucleons − mass of nucleus
- E = Δmc²

Visual elements:
- Mass defect arrow to energy released
- Binding energy holds nucleus together
- More binding energy per nucleon generally means greater stability
- Iron region stability peak concept

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking mass disappeared. Fix: missing mass is converted to binding energy according to E = mc².

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says mass defect, binding energy, nuclear stability, or E = mc².

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch09_Audio_08_Mass_Defect_Binding_Energy.png`

## Poster 9 — Fission, Fusion, and Medical Radiation Bridge

Audio purpose: compare nuclear processes and applications.

Main visual: heavy nucleus splitting vs light nuclei combining, plus PET/radiation icon panel.

Required concepts:
- Fission: heavy nucleus splits
- Fusion: light nuclei combine
- Energy release depends on moving toward greater binding stability

Visual elements:
- Chain reaction visual
- Fusion in stars
- PET uses positron emission conceptually
- Radiation penetration comparison if space allows

MCAT trap strip: Trap: fission and fusion are opposite-looking but both can release energy. Fix: energy releases when products are more tightly bound/stable per nucleon.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says fission, fusion, chain reaction, PET, radiation therapy, or medical isotope.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch09_Audio_09_Fission_Fusion_Medical_Radiation.png`
