# Physics 1 — Kinematics and Dynamics Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 9 posters

Audio journey: begin with motion language, move into kinematic equations and graphs, then forces, free-body diagrams, circular motion, equilibrium, and collisions.

## Poster 1 — Motion Big Picture: Scalars, Vectors, and the Story of Motion

Audio purpose: orient the listener before equations.

Main visual: road map showing position, displacement, distance, velocity, speed, and acceleration.

Required formulas:
- v = Δx/Δt
- a = Δv/Δt

Visual elements:
- Scalar vs vector split
- Distance vs displacement path
- Speed vs velocity arrows
- Acceleration as change in velocity

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating speed and velocity as the same. Fix: velocity has direction; speed does not.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio defines motion basics, scalars, vectors, displacement, velocity, or acceleration.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_01_Motion_Big_Picture.png`

## Poster 2 — Kinematic Equations: Constant-Acceleration Toolkit

Audio purpose: help the listener know which equation to pick.

Main visual: equation decision tree based on missing variable.

Required formulas:
- vf = vi + at
- Δx = vit + 1/2at²
- vf² = vi² + 2aΔx
- Δx = ((vi + vf)/2)t

Visual elements:
- Variable board: vi, vf, a, t, Δx
- Missing-variable selector
- Constant acceleration warning

MCAT trap strip: Trap: using kinematic equations when acceleration is not constant. Fix: these equations require constant acceleration.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says motion equation, constant acceleration, initial/final velocity, or missing variable.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_02_Kinematic_Equations.png`

## Poster 3 — Motion Graphs: Position, Velocity, Acceleration

Audio purpose: make graphs readable during audio examples.

Main visual: stacked x-t, v-t, and a-t graphs.

Required relationships:
- x-t slope = velocity
- v-t slope = acceleration
- v-t area = displacement
- a-t area = change in velocity

Visual elements:
- Slope arrows
- Shaded area blocks
- Increasing/decreasing/constant motion examples

MCAT trap strip: Trap: reading graph height when asked for slope or area. Fix: identify graph operation first.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says slope, area, graph, velocity-time, or acceleration-time.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_03_Motion_Graphs.png`

## Poster 4 — Newton’s Laws and Net Force

Audio purpose: connect forces to acceleration.

Main visual: object with multiple arrows and net force vector.

Required formulas:
- ΣF = ma
- weight = mg

Visual elements:
- Newton’s 1st, 2nd, and 3rd laws
- Net force as vector sum
- Acceleration points with net force

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking motion requires force. Fix: acceleration requires net force; constant velocity means zero net force.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says force, inertia, Newton, net force, or acceleration.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_04_Newtons_Laws_Net_Force.png`

## Poster 5 — Common Forces and Free-Body Diagrams

Audio purpose: give the listener a force-labeling rescue image.

Main visual: block on table with labeled arrows.

Required formulas:
- Fg = mg
- fs ≤ μsN
- fk = μkN
- Fspring = kx

Visual elements:
- Gravity downward
- Normal force perpendicular to surface
- Tension along string
- Friction opposing relative motion
- Spring force restoring direction

MCAT trap strip: Trap: drawing force arrows in the direction of motion automatically. Fix: force direction depends on interaction, not motion alone.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says free-body diagram, friction, tension, normal force, or spring force.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_05_Common_Forces_FBD.png`

## Poster 6 — Inclines and Force Components

Audio purpose: make inclined-plane problems visually automatic.

Main visual: block on incline with mg split into components.

Required formulas:
- mg sinθ down the ramp
- mg cosθ into the ramp
- N = mg cosθ when no other perpendicular force acts

Visual elements:
- Coordinate axes tilted with ramp
- Parallel/perpendicular components
- Friction direction options

MCAT trap strip: Trap: swapping sine and cosine on inclines. Fix: mg sinθ is parallel down the ramp; mg cosθ is perpendicular into the ramp.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says incline, component, parallel, perpendicular, or friction on a ramp.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_06_Inclines_Force_Components.png`

## Poster 7 — Circular Motion and Equilibrium

Audio purpose: connect changing direction with acceleration.

Main visual: object moving in circle with velocity tangent and acceleration inward.

Required formulas:
- ac = v²/r
- Fc = mv²/r
- T = 1/f

Visual elements:
- Tangential velocity arrow
- Inward centripetal acceleration arrow
- Larger radius softens turn
- Equilibrium as ΣF = 0 and Στ = 0 preview

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking centripetal force is a new force. Fix: centripetal force is the net inward force supplied by tension, gravity, friction, or normal force.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says circular motion, centripetal acceleration, radius, frequency, or equilibrium.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_07_Circular_Motion_Equilibrium.png`

## Poster 8 — Momentum, Impulse, and Collisions

Audio purpose: distinguish force/motion questions from momentum conservation questions.

Main visual: two carts colliding before and after.

Required formulas:
- p = mv
- J = FΔt = Δp
- Σpinitial = Σpfinal when external impulse is negligible

Visual elements:
- Elastic vs inelastic collision comparison
- Momentum conserved vs kinetic energy conserved
- Impulse as area under force-time graph

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming kinetic energy is always conserved. Fix: momentum conservation is broader; kinetic energy is conserved only in elastic collisions.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says collision, impulse, momentum, elastic, or inelastic.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch01_Audio_08_Momentum_Impulse_Collisions.png`
