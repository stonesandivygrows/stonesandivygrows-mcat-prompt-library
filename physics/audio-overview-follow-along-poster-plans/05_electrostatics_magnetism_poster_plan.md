# Physics 5 — Electrostatics and Magnetism Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 10 posters

Audio journey: start with charge, electric force, electric field, potential energy, voltage, and special electric-field cases; then move into magnetism and right-hand-rule rescue.

## Poster 1 — Electrostatics Big Picture: Charge, Force, Field, Potential

Audio purpose: orient the listener to the four-level logic of electrostatics.

Main visual: ladder from charge → force → field → potential → energy.

Required formulas:
- F = kq1q2/r²
- E = F/q
- V = U/q

Visual elements:
- Charge creates field
- Field exerts force on other charges
- Potential describes energy per charge
- Direction rules for positive test charge

MCAT trap strip: Trap: mixing field and potential. Fix: electric field is force per charge; electric potential is energy per charge.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces charge, Coulomb force, electric field, electric potential, or energy.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_01_Electrostatics_Big_Picture.png`

## Poster 2 — Charge and Coulomb’s Law

Audio purpose: make inverse-square electric force intuitive.

Main visual: two charges with force arrows and distance ruler.

Required formulas:
- F = kq1q2/r²

Visual elements:
- Like charges repel
- Opposite charges attract
- Double one charge → double force
- Double distance → one-fourth force
- Sign determines direction, magnitude uses absolute values

MCAT trap strip: Trap: forgetting inverse-square distance. Fix: distance changes are squared in the denominator.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says Coulomb, attraction, repulsion, point charges, or inverse square.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_02_Charge_Coulombs_Law.png`

## Poster 3 — Electric Field: Force Map Around Charge

Audio purpose: separate field from force and make direction automatic.

Main visual: positive charge with field arrows outward and negative charge with field arrows inward.

Required formulas:
- E = F/q
- E = kQ/r²
- F = qE

Visual elements:
- Field direction is defined by force on a positive test charge
- Field lines leave positive charges and enter negative charges
- Denser field lines = stronger field
- Test charge logic panel

MCAT trap strip: Trap: using electron force direction as field direction. Fix: field direction follows positive test charge; electrons feel force opposite E.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says field lines, test charge, E-field, or force on a charge.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_03_Electric_Field.png`

## Poster 4 — Electric Potential Energy and Electric Potential

Audio purpose: connect charge position to stored electric energy.

Main visual: charge moving through electric potential landscape.

Required formulas:
- U = kq1q2/r
- V = kQ/r
- U = qV

Visual elements:
- Potential energy belongs to a system of charges
- Electric potential belongs to location/source charge setup
- Positive charges move toward lower potential energy if free
- Electron sign warning

MCAT trap strip: Trap: voltage equals force. Fix: voltage is energy per charge; field is related to force per charge.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says electric potential energy, voltage, potential, or energy per charge.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_04_Electric_Potential_Energy_Voltage.png`

## Poster 5 — Uniform Electric Fields and Parallel Plates

Audio purpose: prepare for membrane/capacitor-style passages.

Main visual: parallel plates with uniform electric field arrows.

Required formulas:
- E = V/d
- F = qE
- U = qV

Visual elements:
- Positive plate to negative plate field direction
- Field strength increases when voltage increases
- Field strength increases when plate separation decreases
- Charge acceleration between plates

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking field depends only on voltage. Fix: between plates, field depends on voltage divided by distance.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says parallel plates, uniform field, membrane potential, capacitor plates, or E = V/d.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_05_Uniform_Field_Parallel_Plates.png`

## Poster 6 — Special Cases and Conductors

Audio purpose: consolidate conductor/equilibrium logic.

Main visual: conducting sphere, charged shell, and electric shielding box.

Required concepts:
- Electric field inside conductor at electrostatic equilibrium is zero
- Excess charge resides on conductor surface
- Equipotential conductor surface

Visual elements:
- Hollow conductor shielding
- Charges on outer surface
- Field lines perpendicular to surface
- Equipotential label

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming charges stay throughout a metal conductor. Fix: at electrostatic equilibrium, excess charge moves to the surface and interior E-field is zero.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says conductor, shielding, equipotential, surface charge, or special case.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_06_Special_Cases_Conductors.png`

## Poster 7 — Magnetism Big Picture: Moving Charges Create Magnetic Effects

Audio purpose: transition from electrostatics to magnetism.

Main visual: moving charge/current wire generating circular magnetic field lines.

Required formulas:
- FB = qvB sinθ
- FB = ILB sinθ

Visual elements:
- Magnetic force acts on moving charges
- Magnetic field direction from right-hand rule
- Force is perpendicular to velocity and magnetic field
- No magnetic force when v is parallel to B

MCAT trap strip: Trap: magnetic fields push stationary charges. Fix: magnetic force requires moving charge or current.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says magnetic field, moving charge, current wire, or perpendicular force.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_07_Magnetism_Big_Picture.png`

## Poster 8 — Right-Hand Rule Rescue

Audio purpose: provide a visual cheat sheet for direction questions.

Main visual: three right-hand-rule panels.

Required formulas:
- F = qvB sinθ
- F = ILB sinθ

Visual elements:
- Moving positive charge: fingers v, curl to B, thumb F
- Current-carrying wire: thumb current, curled fingers B
- Negative charge reverses force direction
- Into/out-of-page symbols

MCAT trap strip: Trap: forgetting to reverse direction for negative charges. Fix: use right hand for positive charge/current, then reverse for electron.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says right-hand rule, direction, into page, out of page, magnetic force, or electron.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_08_Right_Hand_Rule_Rescue.png`

## Poster 9 — Magnetic Force, Circular Motion, and Work Trap

Audio purpose: connect magnetism back to circular motion.

Main visual: charged particle moving in a circular path inside magnetic field.

Required formulas:
- qvB = mv²/r when perpendicular
- r = mv/(qB)

Visual elements:
- Magnetic force perpendicular to velocity
- Curved path but speed unchanged
- Larger mass or velocity → larger radius
- Larger charge or magnetic field → smaller radius

MCAT trap strip: Trap: magnetic force does work and changes speed. Fix: magnetic force is perpendicular to motion, so it changes direction but not speed.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says charged particle path, circular motion, magnetic force, or magnetic work.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch05_Audio_09_Magnetic_Force_Circular_Motion.png`

## Poster 10 — Electrostatics and Magnetism MCAT Trap Board

Audio purpose: final rescue poster for charge/field/potential/direction questions.

Main visual: two-column trap board: electrostatics vs magnetism.

Required formulas: Coulomb’s law, E = F/q, E = V/d, U = qV, FB = qvB sinθ, FB = ILB sinθ.

Visual elements:
- Field vs force vs potential distinction
- Positive test charge direction
- Electron reversal warning
- Inverse-square warning
- Magnetic force perpendicular warning

MCAT trap strip: Trap: signs and directions. Fix: solve magnitude first, then determine direction using charge sign and field/right-hand rules.

Audio-follow cue: use at the end of the audio and before practice sets.
