# Physics 8 — Light and Optics Audio Overview Poster Plan

Recommended total: 10 posters

Audio journey: start with light as electromagnetic radiation, then connect wavelength/frequency/energy, reflection, refraction, mirrors, lenses, image formation, diffraction, polarization, and optical traps.

## Poster 1 — Light and Optics Big Picture: EM Radiation Meets Matter

Audio purpose: orient the listener to light as both a wave and a ray-model tool.

Main visual: electromagnetic wave traveling into mirror, lens, prism, slit, and eye stations.

Required formulas:
- c = fλ
- E = hf

Visual elements:
- Light as electric and magnetic fields
- Ray optics vs wave optics split
- Reflection, refraction, diffraction, polarization preview
- Eye and microscope bridge

MCAT trap strip: Trap: treating light as only a particle or only a ray. Fix: MCAT optics uses both ray behavior and wave behavior depending on the question.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio introduces electromagnetic radiation, light behavior, ray optics, or wave optics.

## Poster 2 — Electromagnetic Spectrum and Photon Energy

Audio purpose: make wavelength, frequency, and energy relationships automatic.

Main visual: EM spectrum from radio to gamma, with visible band expanded.

Required formulas:
- c = fλ
- E = hf
- E = hc/λ

Visual elements:
- Radio → microwave → infrared → visible → ultraviolet → X-ray → gamma
- Red = longer wavelength, lower energy
- Violet = shorter wavelength, higher energy
- Frequency-energy direct relationship
- Wavelength-energy inverse relationship

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking red light is higher energy because it feels visually intense. Fix: violet has shorter wavelength and higher photon energy than red.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says spectrum, wavelength, frequency, photon energy, red, violet, UV, or visible light.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch08_Audio_02_EM_Spectrum_Photon_Energy.png`

## Poster 3 — Reflection and Mirrors

Audio purpose: establish ray-tracing basics before lenses.

Main visual: incident ray and reflected ray on plane, concave, and convex mirrors.

Required formulas:
- θi = θr
- 1/f = 1/o + 1/i
- m = -i/o

Visual elements:
- Law of reflection
- Plane mirror virtual image
- Concave mirror = converging
- Convex mirror = diverging
- Focal point and center of curvature labels

MCAT trap strip: Trap: assuming all mirrors form real images. Fix: convex mirrors always form virtual, upright, reduced images.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says mirror, reflection, focal point, concave, convex, or ray tracing.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch08_Audio_03_Reflection_Mirrors.png`

## Poster 4 — Refraction and Snell’s Law

Audio purpose: make bending toward/away from the normal visual.

Main visual: ray crossing air-water/glass boundary with normal line and angle labels.

Required formulas:
- n = c/v
- n1 sinθ1 = n2 sinθ2

Visual elements:
- Entering higher n slows light and bends toward normal
- Entering lower n speeds light and bends away from normal
- Normal line anchor
- Medium comparison table

MCAT trap strip: Trap: memorizing “toward normal” without checking refractive index direction. Fix: higher n means slower light and bending toward normal.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says refraction, Snell’s law, normal, denser medium, or index of refraction.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch08_Audio_04_Refraction_Snells_Law.png`

## Poster 5 — Total Internal Reflection and Critical Angle

Audio purpose: distinguish refraction from total internal reflection.

Main visual: ray inside glass/water hitting boundary at small, critical, and large angles.

Required formulas:
- sinθc = n2/n1 when n1 > n2

Visual elements:
- Only occurs going from high n to low n
- Critical angle ray travels along boundary
- Larger than critical angle reflects internally
- Fiber optic / endoscopy bridge

MCAT trap strip: Trap: thinking total internal reflection can happen from low n to high n. Fix: it requires travel from higher refractive index to lower refractive index.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says critical angle, total internal reflection, fiber optics, or endoscopy.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch08_Audio_05_Total_Internal_Reflection.png`

## Poster 8 — Dispersion, Chromatic Aberration, and Prism Logic

Audio purpose: preserve the audio distinction between rainbow separation and optical defects.

Main visual: white light through prism splitting into spectrum beside a lens with color-focal-point mismatch.

Required concepts:
- Index of refraction can depend on wavelength
- Shorter wavelength bends more in many materials

Visual elements:
- Prism dispersion
- Violet bends more than red
- Chromatic aberration as lens defect
- Spherical aberration as geometry defect

MCAT trap strip: Trap: mixing dispersion and aberration. Fix: dispersion is wavelength separation; aberration is image distortion/defect.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says prism, rainbow, dispersion, chromatic aberration, spherical aberration, violet, or red.

Suggested filename: `Physics_Ch08_Audio_08_Dispersion_Aberration_Prism.png`

## Poster 9 — Diffraction, Interference, and Polarization

Audio purpose: switch from ray optics to wave optics.

Main visual: single slit spreading, double slit interference bands, and polarized light through filters.

Required relationships:
- Constructive interference depends on path difference
- Polarization filters electric field orientation

Visual elements:
- Single-slit spreading
- Double-slit bright/dark bands
- Diffraction grating separates colors
- Polarizer blocking one field orientation

MCAT trap strip: Trap: expecting a tiny slit to create a tiny sharp dot. Fix: waves spread after narrow openings; that spreading is diffraction.

Audio-follow cue: use when the audio says diffraction, double slit, interference, grating, bright bands, dark bands, or polarization.
