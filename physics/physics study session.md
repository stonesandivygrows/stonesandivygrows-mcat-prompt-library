# MCAT Physics — Waves & Sound Hierarchy Flowchart

WAVES & SOUND
│
├── 1. Wave Identity / Wave Concept
│   ├── What is a wave?
│   │   ├── Transfers energy through space or matter
│   │   ├── Does not require net mass transport
│   │   └── Medium particles oscillate locally; energy moves forward
│   │
│   ├── Does it need a medium?
│   │   ├── Mechanical waves → require a medium
│   │   │   ├── Sound
│   │   │   ├── Rope waves
│   │   │   ├── Water waves
│   │   │   └── Seismic waves
│   │   │
│   │   └── Electromagnetic waves → no medium required
│   │       ├── Radio waves
│   │       ├── Microwaves
│   │       ├── Infrared Radiation
│   │       ├── Light (Visible and Ultraviolet)
│   │       ├── X-rays
│   │       └── Gamma rays
│   │
│   ├── Which direction does the medium move?
│   │   ├── Transverse waves
│   │   │   ├── Medium oscillates ⟂ to wave travel
│   │   │   ├── Can be polarized
│   │   │   └── Examples: rope waves, EM waves
│   │   │
│   │   └── Longitudinal waves
│   │       ├── Medium oscillates ∥ to wave travel
│   │       ├── Compression = high pressure/density
│   │       ├── Rarefaction = low pressure/density
│   │       └── Sound = longitudinal mechanical wave
│   │
│   ├── Is the wave pattern moving or fixed?
│   │   ├── Traveling waves
│   │   │   └── Points of maximum/minimum displacement move with propagation
│   │   │
│   │   └── Standing waves
│   │       ├── Pattern appears fixed in place
│   │       ├── Nodes and antinodes do not move with propagation
│   │       └── Detailed formulas go under Standing Waves & Harmonics
│   │
│   ├── What range of sound frequency?
│   │   ├── Infrasonic waves → below human hearing
│   │   ├── Audible sound → human hearing range
│   │   └── Ultrasonic waves → above human hearing
│   │
│   └── Space example
│       ├── Supernova visible in space ✓
│       ├── Supernova sound in space ✗
│       └── Light travels in vacuum; sound does not
│
├── 2. Core Wave Variables
│   ├── Displacement & amplitude
│   ├── Wavelength
│   ├── Frequency & period
│   └── Wave speed equation
│
├── 3. What Changes When...
│   ├── Amplitude changes
│   ├── Distance changes
│   ├── Relative motion changes
│   ├── Medium changes
│   └── Driving frequency changes
│
├── 4. Intensity & Sound Level
│   ├── Intensity
│   ├── Inverse-square spreading
│   ├── Sound level / decibels
│   └── Loudness
│
├── 5. Doppler Effect
│   ├── Source/observer move toward each other
│   ├── Source/observer move away from each other
│   ├── Zero relative motion
│   └── Pitch change
│
├── 6. Superposition & Interference
│   ├── Superposition
│   ├── Constructive interference
│   ├── Destructive interference
│   └── Phase relationship
│
├── 7. Standing Waves & Harmonics
│   ├── Nodes and antinodes
│   ├── Strings
│   ├── Open pipes
│   └── Closed pipes
│
├── 8. Resonance & Damping
│   ├── Resonance
│   ├── Natural frequency
│   ├── Damping
│   └── Attenuation
│
├── 9. Shock Waves
│   ├── Source reaches/exceeds sound speed
│   ├── Wavefronts pile up
│   └── Sonic boom
│
├── 10. Medical Applications
│   ├── Ultrasound imaging
│   ├── Doppler ultrasound
│   └── Therapeutic ultrasound

└── 11. Major Traps
    ├── Medium vs no medium
    ├── Pitch vs loudness
    ├── dB vs intensity
    ├── Amplitude measurement
    └── Closed-pipe formulas
