# Biology High-Confusion Term Pairs

## Purpose
Maintain a compact correction registry for biology terms that are frequently confused in speech, transcription, diagrams, or rapid MCAT review.

## Blood and immunity

### Plasma vs plasma cell
- Plasma: liquid portion of blood
- Plasma cell: activated B cell that secretes antibodies

### Leukocyte vs lymphocyte
- Leukocyte: any white blood cell
- Lymphocyte: B cell, T cell, or NK cell; a subtype of leukocyte

### Granulocyte vs phagocyte
- Granulocyte: appearance-based category with visible granules
- Phagocyte: function-based category that engulfs material
- Neutrophils are both granulocytes and phagocytes

### Monocyte vs macrophage
- Monocyte: circulating agranulocyte
- Macrophage: differentiated tissue phagocyte derived from monocytes

### Lysozyme vs lysosome
- Lysozyme: antibacterial enzyme in tears and saliva
- Lysosome: acidic intracellular digestive organelle

### Gastrin vs gastric acid
- Gastrin: hormone that stimulates acid secretion
- Gastric acid/HCl: nonspecific innate defense that kills many pathogens

### MHC I vs MHC II
- MHC I: almost all nucleated cells; endogenous antigen; CD8 cytotoxic T cells
- MHC II: professional APCs; exogenous antigen; CD4 helper T cells

## Cardiovascular and renal

### Artery/arteriole vs atrium
- Artery: high-pressure vessel carrying blood away from heart
- Arteriole: major resistance vessel
- Atrium: chamber of the heart

### Lumen vs lining
- Lumen: hollow inner space of a tube
- Endothelium/epithelium: cellular lining around the lumen

### Filtration vs secretion
- Filtration: glomerular blood → Bowman’s space
- Secretion: peritubular blood → tubule after filtration

### Reabsorption vs excretion
- Reabsorption: tubule → blood
- Excretion: tubule/urinary tract → outside body

### ADH vs aldosterone
- ADH: retains water; lowers plasma osmolarity; concentrates urine
- Aldosterone: retains Na+, secretes K+/H+; increases ECF volume and pressure

### Plasma osmolarity vs urine concentration
- High ADH: plasma becomes more dilute while urine becomes more concentrated
- Low ADH: plasma can become more concentrated while urine becomes more dilute

## Respiratory and acid-base

### Partial pressure diffusion vs Starling forces
- Partial pressure gradients move O2 and CO2
- Starling forces move fluid across capillaries

### Boyle’s law vs Venturi effect
- Boyle’s law: inhalation through increased thoracic volume and decreased pressure
- Venturi effect: pressure drop and entrainment caused by faster flow through a narrowing

### Hyperventilation vs hypoventilation
- Hyperventilation: CO2 decreases; respiratory alkalosis
- Hypoventilation: CO2 increases; respiratory acidosis

### Bicarbonate vs carbonate
- HCO3−: bicarbonate
- CO3²−: carbonate

## Reproduction and embryology

### Primary oocyte vs secondary oocyte
- Primary oocyte: arrested in prophase I
- Secondary oocyte: formed after meiosis I; arrested in metaphase II; ovulated

### Secondary oocyte vs ovum vs zygote
- Secondary oocyte completes meiosis II after sperm entry
- Ovum is the completed female gamete
- Zygote forms after fusion of male and female genetic material

### Follicle vs corpus luteum vs corpus albicans
- Follicle: contains developing oocyte before ovulation
- Corpus luteum: formed from ruptured follicle after ovulation; secretes progesterone and estrogen
- Corpus albicans: scar-like remnant after corpus luteum degeneration

### Blastula vs blastocyst
- Blastula: broad embryology term
- Blastocyst: mammalian/human implantation-stage structure

### Blastocoel vs blastopore vs archenteron
- Blastocoel: fluid-filled cavity of blastocyst/blastula
- Blastopore: opening formed during gastrulation
- Archenteron: primitive gut cavity

### Trophoblast vs inner cell mass
- Trophoblast: contributes to placenta/support tissue; secretes hCG after implantation begins
- Inner cell mass: embryo proper and germ-layer source

### Notochord vs neural tube
- Notochord: mesoderm-derived signaling structure
- Neural tube: ectoderm-derived precursor of brain and spinal cord

### Neural tube vs neural crest
- Neural tube: central nervous system
- Neural crest: peripheral nervous system, adrenal medulla, melanocytes, and selected craniofacial structures

### Ectoderm vs mesoderm vs endoderm
- Ectoderm: epidermis and nervous system
- Mesoderm: muscle, skeleton, connective tissue, circulatory system, gonads, adrenal cortex, most excretory structures
- Endoderm: digestive/respiratory epithelium, pancreas, thyroid, bladder/distal urinary epithelium

## Endocrine signaling

### Peptide vs steroid hormone
- Peptide: polar, surface receptor, second messenger, generally fast and short-lived
- Steroid: lipid-soluble, intracellular receptor, transcriptional, generally slower and longer-lasting

### Catecholamine vs thyroid hormone
- Both are amino acid-derived
- Catecholamines act through membrane receptors
- Thyroid hormones act through intracellular receptors

### Tropic vs direct hormone
- Tropic: stimulates another endocrine gland
- Direct/non-tropic: acts mainly on non-endocrine target tissue

### Effector vs receptor
- Receptor: detects the signal
- Effector: final molecule or machine that directly carries out the response

### Existing-protein response vs transcriptional response
- Existing-protein response: relocation or modification of proteins already present
- Transcriptional response: altered gene expression followed by new protein synthesis

## Usage rule
When a transcript contains one of these terms, normalize the wording but preserve the learner’s intended reasoning. Flag the correction explicitly only when the mistaken term would change the biological mechanism.
