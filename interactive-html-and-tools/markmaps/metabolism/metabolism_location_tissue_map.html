Where inside the cell does the pathway physically occur?

Which organs meaningfully perform or rely on the pathway?

Liver Kidney Cortex Muscle Adipose Brain RBCs Heart β-cells
Key example: Glycolysis is cytosolic and broadly available in nearly every cell — but how tissues use glucose differs dramatically. RBCs are glycolysis-only; brain prioritizes glucose but can adapt; liver routes G6P to glycogen, GNG, or PPP.

Section 03
Tissue Capability Table
Tissue ✅ Can Do 🚫 Cannot / Limited Identity Memory Line
🟠 Liver Glycolysis, Gluconeogenesis, Glycogenesis, Glycogenolysis, PPP, FA synthesis, β-oxidation, Ketogenesis, Urea cycle, G6Pase (release free glucose) Does not burn the glucose it makes during fasting; fuels GNG via β-oxidation Public glucose manager "Liver = public bank; shares with all"
🩵 Kidney Cortex Glycolysis, Gluconeogenesis (esp. prolonged fasting), G6Pase present → can release glucose Minor contributor early; ramps up only in prolonged fasting (>24h) Long-fast backup "Kidney helps liver when fast goes long"
🔵 Skeletal Muscle Glycolysis, Glycogenesis, Glycogenolysis (own stores), TCA, ETC, β-oxidation; creatine phosphate burst No G6Pase → CANNOT release free glucose into blood. Glycogen is private; no GNG meaningfully Selfish energy store "Muscle glycogen = private wallet"
🟢 Adipose Glycolysis, FA synthesis, TAG synthesis, Lipolysis; glucose → DHAP → glycerol-3-P (TAG backbone) Cannot release free glucose; limited GNG; no significant β-oxidation export Fat builder & releaser "Adipose uses glucose to build TAG"
🟣 Brain / Neurons Glycolysis, TCA, ETC; GLUT3 (high affinity); uses ketones during prolonged fasting Cannot meaningfully perform GNG; cannot use fatty acids directly (FA don't cross BBB well); ketones only with adaptation Glucose-first adapter "Brain loves glucose; adapts to ketones"
🔴 RBCs Glycolysis ONLY. Also: 2,3-BPG production (regulates O₂ affinity) NO mitochondria → NO TCA, ETC, OxPhos, β-oxidation, ketone use, GNG, PPP (oxidative), FA synthesis Glycolysis-only cell "RBCs: no mito → glycolysis only"
🩷 Heart Glycolysis, TCA, ETC, β-oxidation (preferred!), Ketone use; highly aerobic; dense mitochondria Minimal anaerobic capacity; poor tolerance for ischemia (irreversible damage fast) Aerobic fuel maximizer "Heart loves mitochondria"
🟡 Pancreatic β-cells Glycolysis, TCA, OxPhos; GLUT2 uptake proportional to blood glucose; ATP↑ → K⁺ channel closes → depolarizes → Ca²⁺ entry → insulin exocytosis — Glucose sensor + insulin "β-cells metabolize glucose to sense it"
Section 04
GLUT Transporter Mini-Map
GLUT1
RBCs, BBB, brain endothelium, placenta
Low Km • high affinity • always active
Ensures basal uptake; not insulin-responsive
GLUT2
Liver + Pancreatic β-cells
High Km • low affinity • glucose SENSOR
Uptake ∝ blood glucose → β-cell reads level → insulin release. Liver only takes in glucose when it's truly high.
GLUT3
Neurons (+ gonads, placenta)
Very low Km • very high affinity
Brain monopolizes glucose even at low blood levels; always saturated at physiological range
GLUT4
Muscle + Adipose
Low Km • insulin-responsive
Stored in vesicles; insulin → PI3K/Akt cascade → vesicle fusion → mass insertion into membrane. FAILS in T2DM.

Section 06
⚠ Confusion Correction Corner
A. Liver vs. Muscle Glycogen
Liver = public bank. Has G6Pase. G6P → glucose → blood.
Muscle = private wallet. No G6Pase. G6P stays trapped → must burn locally.
B. TAG Backbone ≠ Free Glycerol
Glycerol-3-phosphate (not free glycerol!) is made from glycolysis:
Glucose → G3P/DHAP → Glycerol-3-P → TAG backbone Note: G3P = glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (glycolysis intermediate)
         Glycerol-3-P = TAG ester backbone ← these are different!
C. F1,6BP → Glycogen? No.
F1,6BP does not directly enter glycogen.
F1,6BP → F6P → G6P → G1P → UDP-glucose → glycogen
D. β-cell Insulin Signal = ATP, not Citrate CORRECTED
Glucose → glycolysis + OxPhos → ATP↑ → K_ATP channel closes → membrane depolarizes → voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels open → Ca²⁺ in → insulin vesicle exocytosis. Not citrate buildup.
E. Urea Cycle: Split Location BONUS
Steps 1–2 (carbamoyl-P + citrulline) occur in mitochondrial matrix.
Steps 3–5 (argininosuccinate → arginine → urea) occur in cytosol.
Section 07 · Final Sticky Memory
Glycolysis
Cytosolic and broadly available in nearly all cells. Key crossroads: G6P can go to glycolysis, glycogen, or PPP.
Gluconeogenesis
Mainly liver; kidney cortex in prolonged fasting. Requires G6Pase to release glucose. Fueled by β-oxidation of FAs.
RBCs
Glycolysis only. No mitochondria → no TCA, ETC, OxPhos, β-oxidation, or ketone use. Always need glucose.
Muscle vs Liver Glycogen
Muscle glycogen = private (no G6Pase). Liver glycogen = public (has G6Pase → releases free glucose to blood).
GLUT2 vs GLUT4
GLUT2 = high Km, low affinity, glucose sensor (liver + β-cells). GLUT4 = insulin-responsive, muscle + fat.
Signals
ATP + citrate = local energy surplus signals (inhibit PFK-1). Insulin / glucagon = whole-body hormonal signals.
Adipose
Uses glucose → DHAP → glycerol-3-P for TAG backbone. Fed: lipogenesis. Fasting: lipolysis → FFA to blood.
ETC Location
ETC complexes are on the inner mitochondrial membrane. H⁺ pumps into intermembrane space. Flows back through ATP synthase.
