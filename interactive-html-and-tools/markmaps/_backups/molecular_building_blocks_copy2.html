Adenosine
Base structure: adenine + ribose sugar
💡 Key Insight: Adenosine is the core!
Adenosine = Adenine base + Ribose sugar
All these molecules (ATP, AMP, cAMP) contain adenosine as their foundation. The differences are in the phosphate groups attached.
ATP
Adenosine + 3 Phosphates
Energy currency of the cell
• Stores energy in phosphate bonds
• Powers most cellular processes
• Hydrolysis releases ~7.3 kcal/mol
High energy Unstable bonds
ADP
Adenosine + 2 Phosphates
Intermediate energy state
• Product of ATP hydrolysis
• Can be recharged back to ATP
• Cellular "spent battery"
Moderate energy Rechargeable
AMP
Adenosine + 1 Phosphate
Low energy nucleotide
• Signals low energy status
• Activates AMPK (energy sensor)
• Building block for RNA
Low energy Signaling RNA component
cAMP
Cyclic AMP (ring structure)
Second messenger signaling
• NOT for energy!
• Mediates hormone signals (epinephrine, glucagon)
• Phosphate forms ring with ribose
Signaling only Hormone response
⚠️ Common Mistake
cAMP is NOT an energy molecule! Even though it contains adenosine like ATP, its function is completely different. cAMP is a signaling molecule, while ATP/ADP/AMP are about energy storage and transfer.

🔑 Key Concept: Ion Gradients
Think of ions like a battery: Na⁺ high outside, K⁺ high inside creates electrical potential. The Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase pump (uses ATP!) maintains these gradients. When ions flow down their gradients, energy is released for signaling.

🧬 Structure of Nucleotides
Every nucleotide has THREE parts:
1. Nitrogenous base (A, T, G, C, or U)
2. Sugar (ribose in RNA, deoxyribose in DNA)
3. Phosphate group(s)

Key difference: DNA uses Thymine (T), RNA uses Uracil (U)
⚠️ Base Pairing Rules
DNA: A pairs with T (2 hydrogen bonds), G pairs with C (3 hydrogen bonds)
RNA: A pairs with U (2 hydrogen bonds), G pairs with C (3 hydrogen bonds)

Mnemonic: "AT" has 2 letters, 2 bonds. "GC" has 3 letters total, 3 bonds.

Understanding the Overlaps

🔗 Adenosine: The Common Thread
Adenosine appears in:
• ATP (adenosine triphosphate)
• ADP (adenosine diphosphate)
• AMP (adenosine monophosphate)
• cAMP (cyclic adenosine monophosphate)
• DNA/RNA as the "A" nucleotide (adenine + sugar + phosphate)

Why? Adenosine is incredibly stable and versatile. Evolution selected it as a building block for both energy molecules AND genetic material.
🧩 Pyrophosphate (PPᵢ) Connection
Pyrophosphate = Two phosphate groups linked together

Released during:
• DNA/RNA synthesis (when nucleotides join)
• ATP formation (AMP + PPᵢ → ATP)
• Many biosynthetic reactions

Why it matters: Pyrophosphate hydrolysis is highly exergonic (releases energy), making reactions irreversible and driving biosynthesis forward.
⚡ The ATP-Nucleotide Connection
ATP is BOTH:
1. An energy currency (what we usually think of)
2. A nucleotide building block for RNA

When ATP is incorporated into RNA, it's no longer storing energy — it's genetic information. The cell uses the SAME molecule for two completely different purposes!
