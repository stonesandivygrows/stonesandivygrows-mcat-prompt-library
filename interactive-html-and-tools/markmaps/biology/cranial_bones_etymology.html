Etymology & Memory Notes
Frontal
Latin: frons, frontis — "forehead"
Forms the forehead and the superior roof of both eye orbits. Contains the frontal sinuses.
Paired at birth, fuses midline by ~age 8. Coronal suture joins it to the parietals.
Front of skull = frontal. Exactly where you already think it is.
Parietal ×2
Latin: paries, parietis — "wall"
Forms the lateral walls and superior dome of the cranium.
Coronal suture (ant.) · Sagittal suture (sup. midline) · Lambdoid suture (post.) · Squamosal suture (inf.).
The side walls of a house protecting what's inside — parietal = wall.
Temporal ×2
Latin: tempus, temporis — "time"
The temples — where hair first turns gray with the passage of time.
Contains the external acoustic meatus (ear canal), mastoid process, styloid process, and forms the TMJ (jaw joint).
Press your temples — time → temples → temporal.
Occipital
Latin: ob + caput — "against the head" (back)
Forms the back and base of the cranium.
Contains the foramen magnum (where the spinal cord exits) and the occipital condyles (articulate with C1).
Fall backward — occipital hits first. It is literally the back of your head.
Sphenoid
Greek: sphen + eidos — "wedge-shaped"
A bat-shaped wedge at the cranial base, visible as "greater wings" in the frontal view.
Contains the sella turcica (houses the pituitary gland). The only bone that articulates with all other cranial bones — the keystone.
A wedge holding everything together — imagine a bat with wings spread across the skull base.
Ethmoid
Greek: ethmos — "sieve / strainer"
Perforated like a kitchen sieve — tiny holes in the cribriform plate let olfactory nerve fibers pass through.
Between the orbits, forms part of nasal septum, medial orbital walls, and the anterior cranial floor.
Holey bone = ethmoid. The holes carry your sense of smell from nose to brain.
