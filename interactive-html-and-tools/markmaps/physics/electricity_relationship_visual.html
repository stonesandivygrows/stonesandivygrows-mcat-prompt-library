Electricity & Magnetism: What Each Thing “Looks Like”

Use this as visual grammar: charges create electric fields; fields exert forces; moving charges are current; current creates magnetic fields; voltage is energy-per-charge that pushes current through resistance.

Key correction: electric potential and voltage are not the same as electric field. Electric field is a push direction. Voltage is an energy height difference per charge.

3) MCAT translation table

Source

Charge creates electric effects

q → E, V, U, F

Stationary charge creates electric field and electric potential. Two charges have electrostatic force and electric potential energy.

Motion

Moving charge creates magnetic effects

moving q → I → B

Current is charge per time. Current produces magnetic field loops.

Circuit

Voltage drives current through resistance

ΔV = IR

Higher voltage means stronger driving energy per charge. Higher resistance means less current for the same voltage.
