Why 2 M AgNO₃ is not enough for 2 M sulfate

Main idea: two Ag⁺ ions are needed for every one SO₄²⁻ ion, but 2 M AgNO₃ only gives 2 M Ag⁺.

Solution 1: 2 M (NH₄)₂SO₄

(NH₄)₂SO₄(aq) → 2 NH₄⁺(aq) + SO₄²⁻(aq)

Each dissolved formula unit gives one sulfate and two ammonium ions.

When mixed: precipitation requires 2 silver ions per sulfate

Conclusion: If the solution contains 2 M sulfate, complete precipitation requires 4 M Ag⁺. But 2 M AgNO₃ gives only 2 M Ag⁺. Therefore, silver is insufficient and sulfate is left over.

One-line MCAT rule

compound molarity × ion subscript = ion molarity

2 M (NH₄)₂SO₄ gives 2 M sulfate because sulfate has subscript 1.
2 M AgNO₃ gives 2 M silver because silver has subscript 1.
But Ag₂SO₄ formation needs 2 Ag⁺ for every 1 sulfate, so 2 M silver only covers 1 M sulfate.
