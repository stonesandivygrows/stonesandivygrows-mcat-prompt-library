Topic 7: Periodic Trends - Why Electron Affinity is Negative

The Confusing Part: Electron Affinity

⚠️ Why EA Seems Backwards

The confusion: EA increases in magnitude across the period, but the VALUES become MORE NEGATIVE

  • Na: EA = -53 kJ/mol
  • Cl: EA = -349 kJ/mol

You might think: "-349 is less than -53, so it's decreasing!"

But NO! The MAGNITUDE (absolute value) is increasing:

  • |-53| = 53
  • |-349| = 349
  • 349 > 53 ✓ Electron affinity is INCREASING

It's increasing, but in the negative direction. Think of it like diving deeper underwater - you're going down (more negative), but you're increasing depth.

Why is Electron Affinity Negative?

Definition of Electron Affinity

Electron Affinity (EA) = Energy change when an atom GAINS an electron

Reaction: X(g) + e⁻ → X⁻(g)

Sign convention in thermodynamics:

  • Energy RELEASED (exothermic) = NEGATIVE ΔE
  • Energy ABSORBED (endothermic) = POSITIVE ΔE

For most atoms, gaining an electron RELEASES energy

  • The atom's nucleus attracts the incoming electron
  • This attraction releases energy as the electron falls into the atom's field
  • Energy is released → exothermic → ΔE is negative
  • Therefore: EA is usually negative

The more negative EA is, the more energy released, meaning:

  • The atom REALLY wants that electron
  • The atom is pulling that electron in strongly
  • Higher effective nuclear charge (Zeff)

The Three Major Periodic Trends

Property What it measures Sign Trend across period →
Electron Affinity (EA) Energy RELEASED when gaining e⁻ Negative (exothermic) More negative (magnitude increases)
Ionization Energy (IE) Energy REQUIRED to remove e⁻ Positive (endothermic) Increases (more positive)
Electronegativity (EN) Tendency to attract e⁻ in bond Dimensionless Increases

Detailed Comparison

Electron Affinity (EA)

Definition: Energy change when atom gains an electron

Process: X + e⁻ → X⁻

Why it's negative:

  • Adding an electron to most atoms releases energy
  • Energy release = negative by thermodynamic convention
  • The MORE negative, the MORE energy released
  • More energy released = atom wants electron more

Across Period 3 (example):

  • Na: EA = -53 kJ/mol (releases 53 kJ)
  • Mg: EA ≈ 0 kJ/mol (barely releases anything)
  • Al: EA = -43 kJ/mol
  • Si: EA = -134 kJ/mol
  • P: EA = -72 kJ/mol
  • S: EA = -200 kJ/mol
  • Cl: EA = -349 kJ/mol (releases 349 kJ!)

Why it increases: Zeff increases → nucleus pulls electrons tighter → more energy released when gaining one

Ionization Energy (IE)

Definition: Energy REQUIRED to remove an electron

Process: X → X⁺ + e⁻

Why it's positive:

  • Removing an electron COSTS energy (you have to rip it away from nucleus)
  • Energy input required = positive by thermodynamic convention
  • The MORE positive, the MORE energy needed
  • More energy needed = electrons held more tightly

Across Period 3:

  • Na: IE = 496 kJ/mol
  • Mg: IE = 738 kJ/mol
  • Al: IE = 578 kJ/mol
  • Si: IE = 787 kJ/mol
  • P: IE = 1012 kJ/mol
  • S: IE = 1000 kJ/mol
  • Cl: IE = 1251 kJ/mol

Why it increases: Zeff increases → nucleus pulls electrons tighter → harder to remove them

Electronegativity (EN)

Definition: Ability to attract electrons in a BOND (not gain/lose, just share unequally)

Process: In a bond like H-Cl, how much does Cl pull the shared electrons?

Why it has no sign:

  • It's not an energy measurement
  • It's a relative scale (Pauling scale: 0 to 4)
  • Dimensionless number representing "pulling power"

Across Period 3:

  • Na: EN = 0.93
  • Mg: EN = 1.31
  • Al: EN = 1.61
  • Si: EN = 1.90
  • P: EN = 2.19
  • S: EN = 2.58
  • Cl: EN = 3.16

Why it increases: Zeff increases → atoms pull bonding electrons harder

The Underlying Cause: Effective Nuclear Charge (Zeff)

All Three Trends Have the Same Root Cause

Zeff = Effective Nuclear Charge = How strongly valence electrons feel the nucleus

Across a period (left to right):

  1. Nuclear charge increases (more protons)
  2. Electrons added to SAME shell (no extra shielding)
  3. Result: Zeff increases
  4. Electrons held more tightly

This causes ALL three to increase:

  • EA increases (more negative): Stronger pull → more energy released when gaining e⁻
  • IE increases (more positive): Electrons held tighter → more energy needed to remove one
  • EN increases: Stronger pull on bonding electrons

Same root cause, three different measurements!

🔑 The Key Insight

Why EA is confusing:

  • It's the ONLY one that's negative
  • It increases in magnitude but the VALUE becomes MORE negative
  • We're not used to "bigger = more negative"

The mental model:

  • IE = energy cost → positive, gets bigger
  • EN = pulling power → dimensionless, gets bigger
  • EA = energy payoff → negative (energy OUT), magnitude gets bigger

Think of EA like diving depths:

  • -10 meters underwater
  • -50 meters underwater
  • -100 meters underwater
  • You're going DEEPER (increasing magnitude)
  • But the numbers are getting MORE NEGATIVE

Or think financially:

  • Getting $10 back (small EA)
  • Getting $100 back (medium EA)
  • Getting $1000 back (large EA)
  • You write it as: -$10, -$100, -$1000
  • More negative = better payoff!
