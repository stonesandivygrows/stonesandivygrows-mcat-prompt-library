Catalytic converterWhat is it?

A catalytic converter is the car exhaust component that uses a solid catalyst to convert toxic exhaust gases into less harmful gases.

CO → CO2
hydrocarbons → CO2 + H2O
NO → N2

MCAT translation: catalytic converter = heterogeneous catalysis because the catalyst is solid while exhaust reactants are gases.

Unwanted sulfur reactionWhy mention SO2/SO3?
SO2 → SO3
SO3 + H2O → H2SO4

This is an unwanted side reaction. It shows that catalysts can speed desired reactions but may also allow side reactions.

Homogeneous catalyst not separatedWhy contamination?

If a homogeneous catalyst cannot be separated from products at the end of a reaction, the reaction already occurred. The problem is purification.

reactants + catalyst → product + catalyst still mixed in

Therefore, the product is contaminated by catalyst. This does not mean the reaction failed to occur.

Released vs separatedKey language trap

Released = molecule leaves a binding site during the mechanism.

E + S → ES → EP → E + P

If product is not released, enzyme turnover decreases.

Separated = lab purification after the reaction mixture already exists.

product + catalyst mixed together → need purification

Not released = mechanism/turnover issue. Not separated = purification/contamination issue.

Surface area and rateIs there an equation?

For solid reactants or solid catalysts, the MCAT idea is usually conceptual, not a formal rate-law equation.

more exposed surface area → more active sites/collisions → faster reaction rate
rate ∝ surface area

For catalytic converters: more porous beads/surface = more gas molecules can contact active sites at once.

Current in circuitMain equation
I = V/R

To increase current, increase voltage or decrease resistance. In the discussed question, replacing the bulb with lower resistance directly increased current.

Wire diameterWhy smaller is worse?
R = ρL/A

Smaller diameter means smaller cross-sectional area A.

smaller diameter → smaller A → larger R → smaller I
Wire temperatureWhy hotter is worse?

For most metal wires:

higher temperature → more atomic vibration → more electron collisions → higher resistance → lower current

Computer overheating is related but not identical. Computers mainly slow by thermal throttling: CPU/GPU lowers speed/voltage to avoid damage.

Ion concentration in cellWhy C was wrong?

In an electrochemical cell, ions in solution participate in redox and help sustain charge transfer.

Y^m+ + electrons → Y(s)

Lower ion concentration usually weakens the cell’s ability to sustain current or changes the cell voltage. It does not directly reduce external circuit resistance.

E = E° − (RT/nF) ln Q
Metal + acidWhat does H2 evolves mean?

Evolves means gas is produced/released/bubbles out.

metal + acid → metal cation + H2 gas

For zinc:

Zn(s) + 2H+(aq) → Zn2+(aq) + H2(g)

Metal is oxidized. H+ is reduced.

Al in HClAnswer pattern
2Al(s) + 6H+(aq) → 2Al3+(aq) + 3H2(g)

Half-reactions:

Al(s) → Al3+(aq) + 3e−      oxidation
2H+(aq) + 2e− → H2(g)       reduction

Correct answer logic: Yes; Al is oxidized and H+(aq) is reduced.

Was passage needed?Passage camouflage

Mostly no. This was close to a content/discrete-style question hidden inside a passage.

metal + HCl → metal oxidized + H+ reduced → H2 gas

The passage only provided extra support if it showed Al reducing Zn2+ to Zn(s).

Al vs Zn evidenceWhy supportive?
Al(s) + Zn2+(aq) → Al3+(aq) + Zn(s)

If Zn(s) forms, Zn2+ gained electrons and was reduced. Those electrons came from Al, so Al was oxidized.

Al donates electrons → Al is oxidized → Al is a strong reducing agent → Al can reduce H+ to H2
Reduction potentialsZn vs Al

If Al reduces Zn2+, then Zn2+/Zn has the higher reduction potential.

Ecell = Ered(cathode) − Ered(anode)
Ered(Zn2+/Zn) > Ered(Al3+/Al)

Approximate values:

Zn2+/Zn:  Ered ≈ −0.76 V
Al3+/Al:  Ered ≈ −1.66 V

Higher Ered = wants electrons more = gets reduced. Lower Ered = gives electrons more easily = gets oxidized.

Power unitsWhy ft·lb/s counts

Power is energy or work per time.

P = W/t = E/t
watt = J/s

Work/energy can be force × distance:

J = N·m
ft·lb = foot-pound = work/energy
ft·lb/s = work/time = power

Trap: ft·lb alone is energy/work; ft·lb/s is power.
