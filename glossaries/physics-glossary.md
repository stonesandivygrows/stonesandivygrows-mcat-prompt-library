## Kinematics & Newton's Laws

**Kinematics Equations (Constant Acceleration)** — v = v₀ + at; x = v₀t + ½at²; v² = v₀² + 2ax; x = (v₀+v)t/2. MCAT: Projectile motion: horizontal (a_x = 0, v_x constant) and vertical (a_y = −g ≈ −10 m/s²) are independent. At maximum height: v_y = 0. Time of flight = 2(v₀sinθ)/g. Range = v₀²sin(2θ)/g (maximum at θ = 45°).

**Newton's First Law (Inertia)** — An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force. MCAT: Equilibrium: ΣF = 0 (translational); Στ = 0 (rotational). Mass is quantitative inertia. Common MCAT applications: objects on frictionless surfaces, satellites in orbit.

**Newton's Second Law** — ΣF = ma (net force = mass × acceleration). MCAT: On an incline angle θ: component along the plane = mg sinθ; normal force N = mg cosθ. For Atwood machine: a = (m₁−m₂)g/(m₁+m₂); T = 2m₁m₂g/(m₁+m₂). Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v²/r (toward center).

**Newton's Third Law** — Forces come in equal and opposite pairs acting on DIFFERENT objects. MCAT: Common trap: gravity on book (Earth pulls book down) and normal force (table pushes book up) are NOT Newton's 3rd law pairs — they act on the same object and happen to be equal in magnitude only when the book is in equilibrium. The actual 3rd law pair of Earth's gravity on book = book's gravity on Earth.

**Friction** — Static: f_s ≤ μ_s N (maximum when on verge of motion). Kinetic: f_k = μ_k N (constant; μ_k < μ_s). MCAT: Normal force N = mg cosθ on an incline (not mg). Friction does not depend on contact area. On verge of sliding on incline: tanθ = μ_s. Friction opposes the direction of relative motion (kinetic) or tendency to move (static).

**Uniform Circular Motion** — Net centripetal force = mv²/r directed inward toward center. MCAT: This net force is provided by real forces (gravity, tension, friction, normal force) — never by a separate "centrifugal force" (that is a fictitious force in the rotating reference frame). Period: T = 2πr/v = 2π/ω. For satellite orbiting Earth: gravitational force provides centripetal force; v_orbital = √(GM/r).

## Energy, Work & Momentum

**Work** — W = Fd cosθ where θ = angle between force and displacement vectors. MCAT: W_net = ΔKE (work-energy theorem). Zero work: force ⊥ displacement (normal force on horizontal surface; centripetal force; tension in circular motion). Negative work: force opposes displacement (friction on sliding object). Power: P = W/t = Fv; unit = watt (W = J/s).

**Kinetic and Potential Energy** — KE = ½mv²; gravitational PE: U_g = mgh; elastic PE: U_e = ½kx² (k = spring constant; x = displacement from equilibrium). MCAT: Conservation of mechanical energy (no non-conservative forces): KE₁ + PE₁ = KE₂ + PE₂. With friction: ΔE_mechanical = −W_friction (energy lost to heat). Spring: at max compression/extension KE = 0, PE = max; at equilibrium position, KE = max, PE = 0.

**Linear Momentum and Impulse** — p = mv; Impulse J = FΔt = Δp (impulse-momentum theorem). Conservation of momentum: Σp_before = Σp_after (when no external net force; holds in all collisions). MCAT: Elastic collision: KE conserved; perfectly inelastic: objects stick (maximum KE lost); head-on elastic collision between equal masses → velocities exchange. Distinguishing elastic/inelastic: elastic = bounces; inelastic = sticks or deforms.

**Torque and Rotational Motion** — τ = rF sinθ = r × F (lever arm × perpendicular force component). MCAT: Rotational equilibrium: Στ = 0. Moment of inertia I: resistance to rotational change (I = mr² for point mass). Rotational KE = ½Iω². Angular momentum L = Iω; conserved when Στ_external = 0. Rolling without slipping: v = rω; total KE = ½mv² + ½Iω².

## Fluids

**Fluid Pressure** — P = F/A; unit: Pascal (Pa = N/m²). Hydrostatic pressure: P = P₀ + ρgh (increases with depth h, fluid density ρ). MCAT: Gauge pressure = P_absolute − P_atm. 1 atm = 101,325 Pa ≈ 760 mmHg ≈ 10 m water column. Pressure at a given depth is the same regardless of the shape of the container (Pascal's principle).

**Pascal's Principle** — An external pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted equally to every point in the fluid. MCAT: Hydraulic press: P₁ = P₂ → F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂ → F₂ = F₁(A₂/A₁). Large piston area → large output force; but the large piston moves a smaller distance. Work in = work out (conservation of energy). Clinical: blood pressure transmitted throughout vasculature.

**Archimedes' Principle** — The buoyant force on an object equals the weight of fluid displaced: F_b = ρ_fluid × V_displaced × g. MCAT: Object floats when F_b = mg_object → ρ_object × V_total × g = ρ_fluid × V_submerged × g → fraction submerged = ρ_object/ρ_fluid. Apparent weight in fluid = true weight − buoyant force. Object sinks if ρ_object > ρ_fluid; floats if ρ_object < ρ_fluid; neutrally buoyant if ρ_object = ρ_fluid.

**Continuity Equation** — For incompressible fluid: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ (flow rate Q = Av = constant). MCAT: As cross-sectional area decreases, flow velocity increases and vice versa. Total blood flow (cardiac output) is conserved through the circulatory system, even though individual vessels vary in caliber and velocity.

**Bernoulli's Equation** — P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant along a streamline (for ideal, incompressible, non-viscous, steady flow). MCAT: As flow velocity increases, pressure decreases (and vice versa). Clinically: stenotic (narrowed) artery → ↑local velocity (Bernoulli) → ↓local pressure, but ↑upstream resistance raises overall systemic BP. Venturi effect: fluid speeds up in constriction → lower pressure → can entrain other fluids (atomizers, nebulizers). Pitot tubes and aircraft wings also use Bernoulli.

**Poiseuille's Law** — Q = πr⁴ΔP / (8ηL); Q = volumetric flow rate, r = tube radius, ΔP = pressure gradient, η = viscosity, L = tube length. MCAT: The r⁴ relationship is the most important: halving the radius → (1/2)⁴ = 1/16 of the original flow rate. Doubling radius → 16× flow. Physiologically: vasoconstriction (↓r) dramatically reduces blood flow; vasodilation (↑r) dramatically increases it. Resistance R = 8ηL/(πr⁴); blood flow Q = ΔP/R.

**Surface Tension and Viscosity** — Surface tension: cohesive force at liquid surface; molecules at surface have fewer neighbors → net inward force → liquid minimizes surface area. MCAT: Pulmonary surfactant (secreted by type II pneumocytes) reduces surface tension in alveoli → prevents alveolar collapse (atelectasis); proportionally more effective in small alveoli (LaPlace pressure P = 2T/r → without surfactant, small alveoli would collapse into larger ones). Viscosity: fluid resistance to shear; blood viscosity increases with ↑hematocrit, ↑plasma proteins, ↓temperature.

## Thermodynamics

**Laws of Thermodynamics** — 0th: thermal equilibrium transitivity (if A in equilibrium with B, and B with C, then A in equilibrium with C) — defines temperature. 1st: ΔU = Q − W (U = internal energy; Q = heat added TO system; W = work done BY system). 2nd: entropy of universe never decreases (ΔS_univ ≥ 0 for any process). 3rd: S → 0 as T → 0 K for perfect crystal. MCAT: 1st law restatements: Q = ΔU + W; if W = 0 (isochoric), Q = ΔU; if ΔU = 0 (isothermal ideal gas), Q = W.

**Thermodynamic Processes** — Isothermal (constant T): for ideal gas, ΔU = 0 → Q = W; W = nRT ln(V₂/V₁). Adiabatic (Q = 0): ΔU = −W; gas cools on expansion. Isobaric (constant P): W = PΔV. Isochoric/isovolumetric (constant V): W = 0; Q = ΔU. MCAT: PV diagram: area under curve = work done by system on surroundings. Clockwise cycle = net work out (heat engine); counterclockwise = net work in (refrigerator/heat pump).

**Heat Engines and Carnot Efficiency** — Maximum theoretical efficiency: e = 1 − T_cold/T_hot (temperatures in Kelvin). MCAT: Always less than 1 (2nd law forbids 100% efficiency; some heat must always be exhausted to cold reservoir). Increasing T_hot or decreasing T_cold improves efficiency. Real engines: diesel > gasoline; steam turbines approach 40%. Carnot engine is reversible and maximally efficient.

**Heat Transfer** — Conduction: Q/t = kA(ΔT/L); requires direct contact; k = thermal conductivity. Convection: heat carried by moving fluid. Radiation: P = εσAT⁴ (Stefan-Boltzmann; works through vacuum; all objects above 0 K radiate). MCAT: In vacuum, only radiation works. Good thermal conductor ≠ good electrical conductor (though metals are usually both). Fat and air are good insulators (low k).

## Waves & Sound

**Wave Properties** — v = fλ (wave speed = frequency × wavelength). Transverse: oscillation perpendicular to propagation direction (EM waves, waves on string). Longitudinal: oscillation parallel to propagation (sound, compression waves). MCAT: Wave speed depends on medium, NOT on frequency or amplitude. Period T = 1/f. Amplitude determines wave intensity (energy); frequency determines pitch (sound) or color (light).

**Superposition and Interference** — Constructive: waves in phase → amplitudes add. Destructive: waves 180° out of phase → amplitudes cancel. MCAT: Standing waves require boundary conditions. String fixed at both ends / pipe open at both ends: resonant frequencies fn = nv/2L (n = 1, 2, 3...); all harmonics present. Pipe closed at one end: fn = nv/4L (n = 1, 3, 5... only odd harmonics). Beats: two waves with slightly different frequencies → beat frequency = |f₁ − f₂|.

**Sound Intensity and Decibels** — Intensity I = P/A (W/m²); decibel scale: β = 10 log(I/I₀) where I₀ = 10⁻¹² W/m² (threshold of hearing). MCAT: Every 10 dB = 10× intensity change. Every 3 dB ≈ 2× intensity change (log rule). Two identical sound sources together: intensity doubles → +3 dB. Intensity ∝ 1/r² from a point source (inverse square law). Normal conversation ≈ 60 dB; pain threshold ≈ 120 dB (10¹² × I₀).

**Doppler Effect** — Moving toward each other: observed frequency > source frequency (compressed waves). Moving apart: f_obs < f_source. f_obs = f_source × (v + v_observer)/(v − v_source) when both move toward each other. MCAT: Memory device: use + in numerator and − in denominator when approaching (gets bigger → higher frequency); flip signs when receding. Clinical: ultrasound Doppler measures blood flow velocity; echo Doppler assesses valvular disease and cardiac function.

## Optics

**Reflection** — Law: θ_incident = θ_reflected (measured from normal to surface). MCAT: Plane mirror: virtual image, upright, same size, same distance behind mirror as object is in front. Concave (converging) mirror: focal point in front; can form real or virtual image depending on object position relative to f.

**Snell's Law and Refraction** — n₁ sinθ₁ = n₂ sinθ₂; index of refraction n = c/v. MCAT: Light entering denser medium (higher n) bends toward normal (angle decreases). Entering less dense medium: bends away from normal. Total internal reflection (TIR): occurs when light goes from denser to less dense medium and θ_incident > θ_critical; sin θ_c = n₂/n₁. Applications: fiber optics, endoscopes, diamond cuts, critical angle in eye (vitreous/cornea).

**Thin Lens Equation** — 1/f = 1/d_o + 1/d_i; magnification m = −d_i/d_o = h_i/h_o. Sign conventions: d_o always positive; d_i positive = real image (same side as outgoing light); d_i negative = virtual image (opposite side from outgoing light); f positive = converging lens; f negative = diverging lens. MCAT: Converging (convex) lens: mnemonic RIO = Real, Inverted, Opposite side from object when d_o > f. Diverging (concave) lens: always VUR = Virtual, Upright, Reduced, same side as object. Power of lens: P = 1/f (diopters; f in meters). Eye: cornea (~40 D) + lens (~20 D adjustable) focuses on retina.

**Mirrors** — Concave mirror (converging): focal length f = R/2 > 0; same equation as lenses. MCAT: Concave mirror with object beyond f: real, inverted image. Object inside f: virtual, upright, magnified (same as converging lens for virtual case). Convex mirror (diverging): f < 0; always produces virtual, upright, reduced image (useful for wide-angle view: rear-view mirrors, security mirrors, side mirrors on trucks). Power of spherical mirror: P = 1/f = 2/R.

**Light and Diffraction** — Dispersion: white light through prism → spectrum; violet (short λ) refracts most (higher n); red (long λ) refracts least. Diffraction: waves bend around corners or through apertures; double-slit: constructive at d sinθ = mλ. MCAT: Shorter wavelength → less diffraction around edges. Larger aperture → less diffraction. Visible light: 400 nm (violet) to 700 nm (red). EM spectrum (low to high energy): radio → microwave → IR → visible → UV → X-ray → gamma.

## Electricity & Circuits

**Coulomb's Law** — F = kq₁q₂/r² (k = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C²). Like charges repel; opposite attract. Electric field: E = F/q_test = kQ/r² (points from + toward −). MCAT: Coulomb's law and gravity both follow inverse-square law. Electric field lines: radiate outward from positive charges; terminate on negative charges; never cross; density indicates field strength.

**Electric Potential** — V = kQ/r (potential from a point charge); unit = Volt. Work done by field: W = qΔV = q(V_i − V_f). MCAT: Electric potential is a scalar (can add/subtract directly). Potential energy: U = qV. Moving positive charge from high to low V is spontaneous (like mass falling downhill). Equipotential surfaces are perpendicular to electric field lines. Capacitor stores charge: C = Q/V.

**Capacitors** — C = Q/V; parallel plate: C = ε₀κA/d (κ = dielectric constant; dielectric ↑ C). Energy stored: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C. MCAT: Series: 1/C_total = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ (same formula as parallel resistors — note the reversal!). Parallel: C_total = C₁ + C₂ (same formula as series resistors). Dielectric insertion: ↑ capacitance (more charge stored at same voltage, or same charge at lower voltage).

**Ohm's Law and Resistance** — V = IR; R = ρL/A (ρ = resistivity, L = length, A = cross-sectional area). MCAT: Series resistors: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ... (same current through each; voltage divides proportionally). Parallel resistors: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂; R_parallel < smallest individual R (same voltage; current divides). Power dissipated: P = IV = I²R = V²/R. At same voltage: higher R → less current → less power (P = V²/R). At same current: higher R → more power (P = I²R).

**Kirchhoff's Laws** — KCL (Current): sum of currents entering a node = sum leaving (charge conservation). KVL (Voltage): sum of voltage changes around any closed loop = 0 (energy conservation). MCAT: Apply KVL to solve for unknown currents and voltages in multi-loop circuits. Batteries: EMF (ideal) − internal resistance (r) voltage drop → terminal voltage V_terminal = EMF − Ir.

**RC Circuits** — Resistor + capacitor in series with a battery. MCAT: Charging: capacitor voltage V_C = V_battery(1 − e^(−t/RC)); initially capacitor acts like a wire (current = V/R, all voltage across R); fully charged = open circuit (no current; all voltage across C). Time constant τ = RC (time to reach ~63% of maximum charge). Discharging: Q = Q₀·e^(−t/RC). Biological applications: cell membrane modeled as RC circuit; time constant determines speed of electrical response.

## Magnetism

**Magnetic Force on Moving Charge** — F = q(v × B); magnitude F = qvB sinθ. MCAT: Magnetic force is always perpendicular to both velocity and B field → does NO work on the charge → cannot change speed, only direction → causes circular motion. For positive charge: use right-hand rule (point fingers in v direction, curl toward B → thumb = F). For negative charge: reverse direction. Cyclotron radius: r = mv/(qB).

**Magnetic Force on Current-Carrying Wire** — F = IL × B; magnitude F = ILB sinθ. MCAT: Two parallel wires carrying current in the same direction attract each other; opposite directions repel. Motor principle: current in magnetic field → force → rotation. Torque on current loop = NIAB sinθ.

**Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction** — ε = −dΦ_B/dt = −N × d(BAcosθ)/dt (Φ_B = magnetic flux). MCAT: Lenz's law: induced current flows in a direction that opposes the change in flux (obeys energy conservation). Applications: electric generators (rotating coil in B field → changing Φ → AC current), transformers (V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂; I₁N₁ = I₂N₂), MRI (proton alignment in static B field + RF pulses → signal detection), eddy currents.

## Atomic & Nuclear Physics

**Photoelectric Effect** — Light strikes a metal → electrons ejected only if photon frequency ≥ threshold frequency (f₀ = φ/h; φ = work function). MCAT: KE_max = hf − φ (kinetic energy of ejected electrons increases with frequency above threshold). Below threshold: no electrons ejected regardless of intensity. Above threshold: ↑intensity → ↑number of electrons (not KE). Einstein's explanation: light behaves as photons (quanta). E = hf = hc/λ; h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s.

**Bohr Model of the Hydrogen Atom** — Electrons occupy discrete circular orbits; energy quantized: E_n = −13.6/n² eV. MCAT: Electron absorbs photon → jumps to higher n level; emits photon → falls to lower n. ΔE = hf = hc/λ. Spectral series: Lyman (n→1, UV); Balmer (n→2, visible: 656nm red, 486nm blue, etc.); Paschen (n→3, IR). Higher energy states have less negative energy (less stable; more easily ionized).

**Radioactive Decay** — Alpha (α) decay: emits ⁴₂He → Z decreases by 2, A decreases by 4; low penetration (stopped by paper or skin). Beta-minus (β⁻): neutron → proton + electron + antineutrino; Z+1, A unchanged; moderate penetration. Beta-plus (β⁺) / positron emission: proton → neutron + positron + neutrino; Z−1, A unchanged. Gamma (γ): high-energy photon emitted from excited nucleus; Z and A unchanged; highest penetration (requires lead or thick concrete). MCAT: First-order kinetics: N(t) = N₀ × (½)^(t/t₁/₂) = N₀e^(−λt); t₁/₂ = 0.693/λ. Half-life is constant regardless of initial amount (1st order kinetics).

**Mass-Energy Equivalence** — E = mc² (c = 3×10⁸ m/s). MCAT: Mass defect: actual nuclear mass < sum of proton + neutron masses → difference converted to binding energy (E = Δmc²). Higher binding energy per nucleon = more stable nucleus. Peak stability at iron-56 (⁵⁶Fe). Fission (heavy nuclei split, e.g., U-235): moves toward iron → releases energy. Fusion (light nuclei combine, e.g., H→He): also moves toward iron → releases energy. Both PET scan (positron annihilation) and nuclear reactions use E = mc².
