# MCAT Biology Glossary
*Created: 2026-07-22 | Focus: B/B section high-yield terms*

---

## Cell Biology

**Organelles** — Nucleus: contains DNA; site of transcription. Rough ER: ribosomes on surface; co-translational glycosylation and protein folding. Smooth ER: lipid synthesis, detoxification (P450), Ca²⁺ storage. Golgi: protein processing (glycosylation, phosphorylation), sorting, and secretion; cis face receives from ER, trans face ships to destination. Mitochondria: double membrane; own circular DNA; site of TCA + ETC; ATP production. Lysosomes: acid hydrolases (pH ~4.8); degrade macromolecules and cellular debris. Peroxisomes: H₂O₂-generating reactions (catalase decomposes it); very-long-chain fatty acid oxidation. MCAT: Signal sequences target proteins to ER; mannose-6-phosphate (M6P) tags target proteins to lysosomes.

**Cell Membrane** — Phospholipid bilayer; fluid mosaic model; selectively permeable. MCAT: Integral proteins span the membrane (transmembrane domains); peripheral proteins associate loosely with the surface. Cholesterol: ↑fluidity at low temperature; ↓fluidity at high temperature (acts as a buffer/stabilizer). Unsaturated fatty acids increase fluidity (kinked tails prevent tight packing).

**Cytoskeleton** — Microfilaments (actin, 7 nm): cell shape, motility, muscle contraction, cytokinesis ring. Intermediate filaments (8–12 nm): structural support, keratins, vimentin, nuclear lamins. Microtubules (tubulin αβ-dimers, 25 nm): mitotic spindle, cilia/flagella (9+2 axoneme), intracellular vesicle transport (kinesin toward + end; dynein toward − end). MCAT: Taxol stabilizes microtubules (anti-cancer); colchicine and vincristine depolymerize microtubules (anti-cancer, anti-gout). Cilia: motile (respiratory tract, fallopian tubes) vs. non-motile (primary cilia, sensory). Kartagener's syndrome: defect in dynein arms → immotile cilia → infertility, situs inversus, bronchiectasis.

**Cell Cycle** — G₁ (growth, decision at restriction point) → S (DNA synthesis, all chromosomes replicated once) → G₂ (growth, preparation) → M (mitosis + cytokinesis). MCAT: Cyclins + CDKs drive transitions. Rb (retinoblastoma protein): tumor suppressor; binds and inhibits E2F transcription factor; phosphorylation by CDK4/6-cyclinD releases E2F → S phase entry. p53: activated by DNA damage → induces p21 (CDK inhibitor) → G₁ arrest, or induces apoptosis. Most cancers involve loss-of-function of tumor suppressors (two-hit hypothesis) or gain-of-function of proto-oncogenes.

**Mitosis** — PMAT: Prophase (chromosomes condense, spindle forms, nuclear envelope breaks down); Metaphase (chromosomes align at metaphase plate; kinetochores attach to spindle microtubules); Anaphase (sister chromatids separate → pulled to poles); Telophase (nuclear envelopes re-form; chromosomes decondense). Then cytokinesis. MCAT: Result: 2 genetically identical diploid daughter cells. Cytokinesis in animal cells: actin-myosin contractile ring (cleavage furrow); plant cells: cell plate formed from Golgi vesicles.

**Meiosis** — Two divisions producing 4 haploid gametes from 1 diploid cell. MCAT: Meiosis I: homologous chromosomes pair (synapsis), crossing over occurs (prophase I; chiasmata = sites of crossing over); homologs separate (reduction division). Meiosis II: sister chromatids separate (like mitosis). Independent assortment (random alignment at metaphase I) + crossing over → genetic variation. Nondisjunction: failure of chromosomes to separate properly → aneuploid gametes. Examples: Down syndrome (trisomy 21), Turner (45,X), Klinefelter (47,XXY), Patau (trisomy 13), Edwards (trisomy 18).

## Genetics

**Mendelian Inheritance** — Law of segregation: two alleles of each gene separate during gamete formation (each gamete receives one allele). Law of independent assortment: genes on different chromosomes (or far apart on same chromosome) assort independently into gametes. MCAT: Monohybrid Aa × Aa: 1:2:1 genotypic; 3:1 phenotypic ratio (complete dominance). Dihybrid AaBb × AaBb: 9:3:3:1 phenotypic ratio.

**Incomplete Dominance vs. Codominance** — Incomplete: heterozygote has intermediate phenotype (red × white → pink; 1:2:1 phenotypic ratio). Codominance: both alleles fully expressed (AB blood type shows both A and B antigens). MCAT: ABO blood groups: I^A and I^B are codominant; i is recessive. Type O (ii): universal RBC donor (no surface antigens). Type AB: universal RBC recipient (has both A and B antigens; no antibodies against either).

**Sex-Linked Traits** — X-linked recessive: more common in males (hemizygous; only one X copy). MCAT: Affected sons get X^a from carrier mothers; daughters must be homozygous X^aX^a to be affected. Common X-linked recessive: hemophilia A (factor VIII), hemophilia B (factor IX), red-green color blindness, G6PD deficiency, Duchenne/Becker muscular dystrophy. X-linked dominant (rare): Rett syndrome, fragile X (technically trinucleotide repeat). Y-linked: father → all sons only.

**Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium** — In non-evolving population: p + q = 1; p² + 2pq + q² = 1 (where p = dominant allele frequency, q = recessive allele frequency). MCAT: Strategy: identify q² (recessive phenotype frequency from problem) → √q² = q → p = 1 − q → carrier frequency = 2pq. Five assumptions: no selection, no mutation, no genetic drift, random mating, no migration. Deviations indicate evolutionary forces are acting.

**Gene Linkage** — Genes on same chromosome are linked; may be separated by crossing over during meiosis I. MCAT: Recombination frequency = map distance in centimorgans (cM); 1 cM = 1% recombination. Genes >50 cM apart behave as if unlinked (independent assortment). Genes close together on same chromosome are inherited together more often (linked).

## Microbiology

**Bacteria** — Prokaryotes: no membrane-bound nucleus; circular chromosome; 70S ribosomes (30S + 50S subunits). MCAT: Gram+ bacteria: thick peptidoglycan cell wall, no outer membrane → stain purple (retain crystal violet). Gram−: thin peptidoglycan + outer membrane (with LPS/endotoxin) → stain pink (lose crystal violet; take safranin counterstain). β-lactam antibiotics (penicillin, cephalosporins) inhibit peptidoglycan cross-linking (transpeptidase). Bactericidal vs. bacteriostatic distinction: bactericidal kills; bacteriostatic inhibits growth.

**Bacterial Genetics** — Three mechanisms of horizontal gene transfer: transformation (uptake of naked environmental DNA; requires competence); transduction (bacteriophage carries bacterial DNA: generalized or specialized); conjugation (direct cell-to-cell DNA transfer via pilus; F factor/plasmid). MCAT: All three spread antibiotic resistance genes. Conjugation requires direct contact and the F factor (F+ transfers plasmid to F−; Hfr integrates F into chromosome and transfers chromosomal genes at high frequency).

**Viruses** — Obligate intracellular parasites; require host cell machinery for replication. MCAT: Structure: nucleic acid (DNA or RNA; ss or ds; + or − sense) + protein capsid; ± lipid envelope (derived from host membrane). Lytic cycle: virus replicates → lyses host. Lysogenic cycle: phage DNA integrates as prophage → dormant until induced into lytic cycle. HIV: enveloped ssRNA (+sense) retrovirus; reverse transcriptase; integrase; protease; CD4+ T cell tropism. Influenza: segmented −ssRNA; antigenic drift (mutations) vs. shift (reassortment = pandemic risk).

**Innate Immunity** — Non-specific, rapid (minutes-hours), no memory. MCAT: Physical barriers: skin, mucus, cilia, stomach acid. Pattern recognition: toll-like receptors (TLRs) on macrophages/dendritic cells detect PAMPs (pathogen-associated molecular patterns). Key cells: neutrophils (first responders; phagocytosis; short-lived), macrophages (phagocytosis, antigen presentation; also M1 pro-inflammatory/M2 anti-inflammatory), NK cells (kill virus-infected or tumor cells lacking MHC I without prior sensitization), dendritic cells (professional antigen-presenting cells; bridge innate and adaptive). Complement cascade: C3b = opsonization; C5a = chemoattractant; C5b-9 = membrane attack complex (MAC).

**Adaptive Immunity** — Specific, slower (days), immunological memory (basis of vaccination). MCAT: B cells: produce antibodies; activated by antigen + T helper cell signals. Classes: IgM (first; pentamer; good agglutinator; no memory), IgG (most abundant; crosses placenta; long-lived), IgA (dimer; mucosa; breast milk), IgE (allergy; parasites; mast cell activation), IgD (B cell surface receptor). T cells: CD4+ helper T cells recognize antigen on MHC II (only on APCs); CD8+ cytotoxic T cells recognize antigen on MHC I (on ALL nucleated cells). Two-signal model for T cell activation: antigen on MHC + co-stimulatory signal (B7-CD28); without signal 2 → anergy (tolerance).

## Nervous System

**Resting Membrane Potential** — ~−70 mV (inside negative); maintained by Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase (pumps 3 Na⁺ out, 2 K⁺ in using ATP). MCAT: At rest, K⁺ leak channels are open → K⁺ diffuses out down concentration gradient → inside becomes negative. Equilibrium potentials: K⁺ ≈ −90 mV (inside negative); Na⁺ ≈ +60 mV (inside positive if only Na⁺ determined potential).

**Action Potential** — Threshold (~−55 mV): voltage-gated Na⁺ channels open rapidly → Na⁺ rushes in → depolarization to ~+40 mV. Na⁺ channels inactivate; voltage-gated K⁺ channels open (delayed) → K⁺ flows out → repolarization. Transient hyperpolarization (undershoot, ~−80 mV) before return to rest. MCAT: All-or-nothing principle; amplitude and duration are constant regardless of stimulus strength. Refractory period: absolute (Na⁺ channels inactivated, cannot generate AP); relative (requires stronger-than-normal stimulus). Conduction velocity increases with myelination (saltatory conduction) and larger axon diameter.

**Synaptic Transmission** — AP reaches axon terminal → Ca²⁺ channels open → Ca²⁺ influx → vesicle fusion → neurotransmitter release into synaptic cleft → diffusion → postsynaptic receptor binding → EPSP (excitatory: depolarizes) or IPSP (inhibitory: Cl⁻ in, or K⁺ out → hyperpolarizes). MCAT: Summation: temporal (multiple APs from one neuron) or spatial (simultaneous inputs from multiple neurons). Acetylcholine: nicotinic (ionotropic; skeletal muscle NMJ, autonomic ganglia); muscarinic (metabotropic/GPCR; cardiac, smooth muscle, glands). ACh destroyed by acetylcholinesterase (target of organophosphates, nerve agents).

**Autonomic Nervous System (ANS)** — Sympathetic ("fight or flight"): thoracolumbar origin; short preganglionic (ACh/nicotinic) → paravertebral ganglion → long postganglionic (NE/adrenergic) → effector. MCAT: Sympathetic effects: ↑HR, ↑BP, bronchodilation, ↓GI motility, pupil dilation (mydriasis), glycogenolysis, piloerection. Parasympathetic ("rest and digest"): craniosacral origin; long preganglionic (ACh/nicotinic) → ganglion near/in organ → short postganglionic (ACh/muscarinic) → effector. Effects: ↓HR, ↑GI motility, bronchoconstriction, pupil constriction (miosis), increased secretions, erection. Both divisions use ACh at ganglia (nicotinic); differ in postganglionic neurotransmitter.

## Endocrine System

**Hormone Classes** — Lipid-soluble (steroids, thyroid hormones T₃/T₄, vitamin D): cross plasma membrane freely → bind intracellular or nuclear receptors → directly regulate gene transcription (slow; hours). Water-soluble (peptides, proteins, catecholamines): cannot cross membrane → bind surface receptors → second messengers (fast; seconds-minutes). MCAT: Epinephrine/NE = catecholamines = water-soluble despite being "amines" → membrane receptor (β-adrenergic → Gs → cAMP). Thyroid hormones = amines but lipid-soluble → nuclear receptor (exception!).

**Hypothalamus-Pituitary Axis** — Hypothalamus releases releasing hormones → anterior pituitary releases tropic hormones → target glands → negative feedback on hypothalamus and pituitary. MCAT: Anterior pituitary: ACTH (cortisol), FSH/LH (gonadal hormones), TSH (thyroid hormones), GH, prolactin. Posterior pituitary stores/releases (but doesn't synthesize): ADH/vasopressin (water reabsorption in collecting duct; deficiency → diabetes insipidus) and oxytocin (uterine contraction, milk letdown; positive feedback loop during labor).

**Key Hormone Effects** — PTH (chief cells of parathyroid): ↑Ca²⁺ via bone resorption (osteoclasts), kidney Ca²⁺ reabsorption, and activating vitamin D (→ intestinal Ca²⁺ absorption); NET EFFECT: lowers phosphate (kidney excretion exceeds bone release contribution). MCAT: PTH ↑Ca²⁺ and ↓phosphate — classic test trap. Calcitonin (C cells of thyroid): ↓Ca²⁺ via inhibiting osteoclasts. Aldosterone: ↑Na⁺ reabsorption + ↑K⁺ excretion at collecting duct → ↑blood volume and BP. Cortisol: gluconeogenesis, anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, protein catabolism. ADH: ↑water reabsorption in collecting duct (inserts aquaporin-2 channels); ↓urine volume.

## Cardiovascular & Respiratory

**Cardiac Cycle** — SA node (intrinsic pacemaker, 70 bpm) → AV node (0.1-second delay) → Bundle of His → Left/right bundle branches → Purkinje fibers → ventricular myocardium. MCAT: Systole = ventricular contraction; diastole = ventricular relaxation. S1 = AV valves close (beginning of systole); S2 = semilunar valves close (end of systole). Frank-Starling law: more venous return (preload) → more stretch → stronger contraction → more stroke volume.

**Blood Pressure Regulation** — MAP ≈ DBP + 1/3(SBP−DBP); MAP = CO × TPR; CO = HR × SV. MCAT: Baroreceptors in carotid sinus/aortic arch detect stretch (high BP) → parasympathetic → ↓HR (short-term). RAAS: low BP/renal perfusion → renin release → angiotensinogen → Ang I → ACE (in lung) → Ang II → vasoconstriction (↑TPR) + aldosterone release (↑Na⁺/H₂O retention) → ↑BP. ANP (atrial natriuretic peptide): released by atria when stretched → ↓BP by ↑Na⁺ excretion.

**Lung Volumes** — Tidal volume (TV ~500 mL): normal quiet breathing. IRV: maximum additional inspiration above TV. ERV: maximum additional expiration below TV. RV (~1.2 L): air remaining after maximal expiration (cannot be forcibly exhaled). IC = TV + IRV; FRC = ERV + RV; VC = IRV + TV + ERV; TLC = VC + RV. MCAT: Spirometry: FEV₁/FVC ratio — obstructive disease (asthma, COPD) <0.8 (both decreased but FEV₁ disproportionately reduced); restrictive disease (pulmonary fibrosis) >0.8 (both reduced but proportionally).

**O₂ and CO₂ Transport** — O₂: 98% bound to hemoglobin (4 binding sites; cooperative; sigmoidal curve); 2% dissolved in plasma. CO₂: 70% as HCO₃⁻ (carbonic anhydrase in RBCs: CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻; chloride shift); 23% bound to Hb (carbaminohemoglobin); 7% dissolved. MCAT: Bohr effect: ↑CO₂ or ↑H⁺ (acidosis) or ↑2,3-BPG or ↑temperature → Hb curve shifts right → O₂ released more readily at tissues.

## Renal System

**Nephron Function** — Glomerulus/Bowman's capsule: filtration (GFR ~125 mL/min). Proximal convoluted tubule (PCT): bulk reabsorption of Na⁺, glucose (SGLT2), amino acids, water, HCO₃⁻; secretion of H⁺ and organic acids; isotonic reabsorption. Loop of Henle: descending limb (water permeable, solute impermeable → concentrates filtrate); ascending limb (impermeable to water; Na⁺/K⁺/2Cl⁻ cotransporter pumps solutes out → dilutes filtrate; generates medullary gradient). DCT: aldosterone-sensitive Na⁺ reabsorption, K⁺/H⁺ secretion. Collecting duct: ADH-sensitive water reabsorption; final urine concentration adjustment. MCAT: Free water clearance, GFR using creatinine clearance, and handling of glucose (spills into urine above threshold ~180 mg/dL in diabetes).

**Acid-Base Balance** — Normal arterial pH: 7.35–7.45; PaCO₂: 35–45 mmHg; HCO₃⁻: 22–26 mEq/L. MCAT: Respiratory acidosis (↑CO₂): hypoventilation → renal compensation (↑HCO₃⁻ retention; slow, hours-days). Metabolic acidosis (↓HCO₃⁻): respiratory compensation (hyperventilation, ↓CO₂; fast, minutes). Respiratory alkalosis (↓CO₂): hyperventilation. Metabolic alkalosis (↑HCO₃⁻): vomiting (lose HCl). Henderson-Hasselbalch for blood: pH = 6.1 + log([HCO₃⁻] / 0.03 × PaCO₂).

## Reproductive & Developmental Biology

**Gametogenesis** — Spermatogenesis: continuous from puberty in testes; 1 primary spermatocyte → 4 equal spermatids → 4 functional sperm (equal cytokinesis). Oogenesis: arrested at prophase I from fetal development; primary oocyte completes meiosis I at ovulation → secondary oocyte (arrested at metaphase II) + first polar body; meiosis II completed only at fertilization → ovum + second polar body. MCAT: Key distinction: males produce 4 viable gametes per meiosis; females produce 1 viable egg + polar bodies.

**Embryonic Germ Layers** — Ectoderm: skin epidermis, CNS and PNS, lens of eye, cornea, inner ear, adrenal medulla, enamel of teeth. Mesoderm: all muscle types, bone, cartilage, connective tissue, cardiovascular system, kidneys and gonads, adrenal cortex, spleen. Endoderm: epithelial lining of GI tract, respiratory tract, thyroid and parathyroid glands, liver (parenchyma), pancreas, urinary bladder. MCAT: Neural crest cells (derivatives of ectoderm at neural tube margins): PNS ganglia, Schwann cells, melanocytes, craniofacial bones/cartilage, adrenal medulla, smooth muscle of GI wall, odontoblasts (teeth dentin).
