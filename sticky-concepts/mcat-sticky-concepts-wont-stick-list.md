## 5. Isolated System
**Original card/question:** What is an isolated system?  
**Answer I keep missing:** A system that cannot exchange *energy or matter* with surroundings.  
**Why it matters:** Since no energy or matter crosses the boundary, the total change in internal energy is *ΔU = 0*.  
**Visual I need later:** A sealed box with no arrows crossing in or out.  
**Memory hook:** Isolated = nothing in, nothing out.

## 6. Mechanical Advantage
**Original card/question:** What is mechanical advantage for a lever, ramp, and spool?  
**Answer I keep missing:**  
- Lever: *MA = L_in / L_out*  
- Ramp: *MA = d / h*  
- Spool: *MA = r_in / r_out*  

**Why it matters:** These are geometric versions of mechanical advantage.

**Difference from force definition:**  
- *MA = F_out / F_in* is the force definition.  
- The distance/length/radius formulas come from conservation of work.

**Core relationship:**  
*F_in d_in = F_out d_out*  
So:  
*F_out / F_in = d_in / d_out*

**Visual I need later:** Lever, ramp, and spool diagrams showing input distance vs output distance.  
**Memory hook:** More distance in → more force out.

## 8. Critical Speed in Fluids
**Original card/question:** What is the critical speed of fluid exiting a hole, and where does it come from?  
**Answer I keep missing:** *v = √(2gh)*  

**Why it matters:** This describes the speed of fluid exiting a hole at depth *h* below the surface.

**Where it comes from:**  
It comes from energy conservation:

*mgh = ½mv²*  

Mass cancels:

*v = √(2gh)*

**Key intuition:**  
This is basically free fall in disguise. Fluid lower in the tank has more pressure because of the height of fluid above it, so it exits faster.

**Visual I need later:** Tank of water with a hole at depth h, showing the exiting fluid speed.  
**Memory hook:** Fluid falling height → free fall velocity.
