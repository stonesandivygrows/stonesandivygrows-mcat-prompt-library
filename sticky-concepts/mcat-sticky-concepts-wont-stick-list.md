## 1. Aconitase requires Fe²⁺ / iron-sulfur cluster
**Original card/question:** Describe the second step of the Citric Acid Cycle.  
**Answer I keep missing:** Aconitase is a *metalloprotein* that requires *Fe²⁺* / an iron-sulfur cluster.  
**Why it matters:** The iron-sulfur cluster helps bind and position citrate so aconitase can rearrange it into isocitrate.  
**Visual I need later:** Citrate → cis-aconitate → isocitrate with aconitase holding the molecule using an Fe-S cluster.  
**Memory hook:** Aconitase “irons out” citrate into isocitrate.

## 2. Units of Enthalpy (H)
**Original card/question:** What are the units of enthalpy?  
**Answer I keep missing:** Enthalpy is usually expressed in *kilojoules (kJ)*.  
**Why it matters:** Other thermodynamic properties, like entropy, are often in *joules (J)*, so unit conversion matters in equations like ΔG = ΔH − TΔS.  
**Visual I need later:** ΔG equation with ΔH in kJ and ΔS in J/K, showing the conversion step.  
**Memory hook:** H = Huge → kJ.

## 3. Volume Conversion: Cubic Meters to Liters
**Original card/question:** How many liters are in 1 cubic meter?  
**Answer I keep missing:** *1 m³ = 1000 L*  
**Why it matters:** Cubed units scale by powers of 10. This shows up in gas laws, density, and fluids.  
**Visual I need later:** A 1 m × 1 m × 1 m cube split into 1000 liter-sized cubes.  
**Memory hook:** Big cube = 1000 L.

## 4. Gas Constant R vs Boltzmann Constant k
**Original card/question:** How are R and k related?  
**Answer I keep missing:** *R = N_Ak*, where *N_A = 6.02 × 10²³ molecules/mol*.  
**Why it matters:** R is per *mole*, while k is per *molecule*.  
**Visual I need later:** PV = nRT beside PV = NkT, showing that R is k scaled up by Avogadro’s number.  
**Memory hook:** R = k × Avogadro.

## 5. Isolated System
**Original card/question:** What is an isolated system?  
**Answer I keep missing:** A system that cannot exchange *energy or matter* with surroundings.  
**Why it matters:** Since no energy or matter crosses the boundary, the total change in internal energy is *ΔU = 0*.  
**Visual I need later:** A sealed box with no arrows crossing in or out.  
**Memory hook:** Isolated = nothing in, nothing out.

## 6. Mechanical Advantage
**Original card/question:** What is mechanical advantage for a lever, ramp, and spool?  
**Answer I keep missing:**  
- Lever: *MA = L_in / L_out*  
- Ramp: *MA = d / h*  
- Spool: *MA = r_in / r_out*  

**Why it matters:** These are geometric versions of mechanical advantage.

**Difference from force definition:**  
- *MA = F_out / F_in* is the force definition.  
- The distance/length/radius formulas come from conservation of work.

**Core relationship:**  
*F_in d_in = F_out d_out*  
So:  
*F_out / F_in = d_in / d_out*

**Visual I need later:** Lever, ramp, and spool diagrams showing input distance vs output distance.  
**Memory hook:** More distance in → more force out.

## 7. Entropy of a Reversible Process
**Original card/question:** How do you calculate change in entropy for a reversible process?  
**Answer I keep missing:** *ΔS = Q_rev / T*  
**Why it matters:** This is the core definition of entropy for a reversible process.  
**Visual I need later:** Heat entering a system at constant temperature, increasing entropy.  
**Memory hook:** Entropy = reversible heat spread over temperature.

## 8. Critical Speed in Fluids
**Original card/question:** What is the critical speed of fluid exiting a hole, and where does it come from?  
**Answer I keep missing:** *v = √(2gh)*  

**Why it matters:** This describes the speed of fluid exiting a hole at depth *h* below the surface.

**Where it comes from:**  
It comes from energy conservation:

*mgh = ½mv²*  

Mass cancels:

*v = √(2gh)*

**Key intuition:**  
This is basically free fall in disguise. Fluid lower in the tank has more pressure because of the height of fluid above it, so it exits faster.

**Visual I need later:** Tank of water with a hole at depth h, showing the exiting fluid speed.  
**Memory hook:** Fluid falling height → free fall velocity.
