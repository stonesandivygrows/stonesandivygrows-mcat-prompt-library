# MCAT Sign Conventions: Negative Does Not Always Mean the Same Thing

## Core rule

**Negative does not automatically mean “bad,” “lower,” or “opposite direction.”**  
It depends on the context.

Also:

**Energy is usually a scalar, not a vector.**  
It can be positive or negative, but that does **not** make it a vector.

---

## 1. Energy levels in atoms

\[
E_n=-\frac{13.6}{n^2}\text{ eV}
\]

For hydrogen:

\[
n=1: -13.6\text{ eV}
\]

\[
n=3: -1.51\text{ eV}
\]

Even though both are negative:

\[
-1.51 > -13.6
\]

So **n = 3 is higher energy than n = 1**.

**MCAT rule:**  
Less negative = higher energy.

---

## 2. Electric potential vs electric potential energy

\[
U=qV
\]

For a **positive charge**:

\[
U \text{ and } V \text{ move together}
\]

For an **electron**:

\[
q=-e
\]

So:

\[
U=(-e)V
\]

That means **electron potential energy and voltage move opposite directions**.

**MCAT rule:**  
For electrons, do not treat voltage and energy as the same thing.

---

## 3. Work

\[
W=Fd\cos\theta
\]

Work can be:

- **positive** if force helps motion
- **negative** if force opposes motion
- **zero** if force is perpendicular to motion

**Negative work does not mean “negative energy object.”**  
It means energy is being removed from that object/system.

Example: friction does negative work on a sliding block.

---

## 4. Potential energy

Potential energy depends on the reference point.

For gravity near Earth:

\[
U=mgh
\]

Usually higher height = higher potential energy.

But in electrostatics or atoms, potential energy can be negative because zero is defined at infinite separation.

**MCAT rule:**  
Negative potential energy often means “bound” or “attracted relative to zero at infinity.”

---

## 5. Gibbs free energy

\[
\Delta G < 0
\]

means **spontaneous/exergonic**.

\[
\Delta G > 0
\]

means **nonspontaneous/endergonic**.

This is not about direction in space. It is about thermodynamic favorability.

**MCAT rule:**  
Negative \(\Delta G\) = energy-releasing/favorable direction.

---

## 6. Voltage / cell potential

\[
\Delta G=-nFE
\]

So:

\[
E_{\text{cell}}>0 \Rightarrow \Delta G<0
\]

A positive cell potential means the redox reaction is spontaneous.

**MCAT rule:**  
For electrochemistry, positive voltage pairs with negative Gibbs free energy.

---

## 7. Heat and enthalpy

\[
\Delta H<0
\]

means **exothermic**: heat released.

\[
\Delta H>0
\]

means **endothermic**: heat absorbed.

**MCAT rule:**  
Negative enthalpy means heat leaves the system.

---

## Final memory rule

When you see a negative sign, ask:

**Negative relative to what?**

- Atomic energy: relative to free electron at infinity
- Work: energy transfer direction
- Gibbs free energy: spontaneity
- Enthalpy: heat flow
- Voltage: energy per charge
- Electron voltage problems: sign flips because electron charge is negative

**Do not interpret all negative signs the same way.**
