Core Vocabulary Before the Map

TermMeaningMCAT correction
PresynapticThe cell/axon terminal releasing neurotransmitter at one specific synapse.Not automatically CNS. It means “before this synapse.”
PostsynapticThe cell/target receiving neurotransmitter at one specific synapse.Not automatically PNS or target organ. It means “after this synapse.”
PreganglionicNeuron whose cell body is in the CNS and whose axon goes to a ganglion.“Pre-ganglionic” = before the ganglion, not the same as “presynaptic” in every context.
PostganglionicNeuron whose cell body is in an autonomic ganglion and whose axon goes to the target organ.This neuron is postsynaptic at the ganglion but presynaptic at the target organ.
NucleusCluster of neuron cell bodies in the CNS.Exception: “basal ganglia” are CNS structures historically called ganglia.
GanglionCluster of neuron cell bodies in the PNS.Dorsal root ganglion = sensory; autonomic ganglia = sympathetic/parasympathetic relay.
TractBundle of axons in the CNS.CNS axon bundle.
NerveBundle of axons in the PNS.PNS axon bundle.

Why One Neuron Can Be Both Post and Pre

Synapse #1: Preganglionic neuron → Postganglionic neuron PRESYNAPTIC POSTSYNAPTIC releases ACh receives ACh Synapse #2: Postganglionic neuron → Target organ PRESYNAPTIC POSTSYNAPTIC releases ACh or NE receives ACh or NE Therefore: The postganglionic neuron is POSTSYNAPTIC at the ganglion, but PRESYNAPTIC at the target organ.
Common mistake: “Sympathetic postsynaptic neurons send epinephrine.”
Correction: Most sympathetic postganglionic neurons release norepinephrine at the target. Epinephrine mainly comes from the adrenal medulla and travels through the blood.

Final One-Line Rule

Pre/post-synaptic = role at a synapse. Preganglionic/postganglionic = position relative to a ganglion.
