# Autonomic Neurotransmission and Synaptic Position

## Core distinction

**Presynaptic/postsynaptic** describes a cell's role relative to one specific synapse.

- **Presynaptic cell:** releases neurotransmitter from vesicles.
- **Postsynaptic cell:** receives neurotransmitter through receptors.
- The same neuron can be postsynaptic at one synapse and presynaptic at the next.

**Preganglionic/postganglionic** describes a neuron's position relative to an autonomic ganglion.

- **Preganglionic neuron:** cell body in the CNS; axon travels to an autonomic ganglion.
- **Postganglionic neuron:** cell body in the autonomic ganglion; axon travels to the target organ.

## Universal autonomic relay

```text
CNS
  ↓
Preganglionic neuron
  ↓ releases ACh
Autonomic ganglion
  ↓
Postganglionic neuron
  ↓ releases ACh or NE
Target organ
```

At the ganglion:

```text
Preganglionic neuron = presynaptic
Postganglionic neuron = postsynaptic
```

At the target organ:

```text
Postganglionic neuron = presynaptic
Target organ = postsynaptic
```

Therefore, the postganglionic neuron is **postsynaptic at the ganglion** but **presynaptic at the target organ**.

## Parasympathetic pathway

```text
CNS
  ↓
Preganglionic neuron
  ↓ ACh
Terminal ganglion near/in target
  ↓
Postganglionic neuron
  ↓ ACh
Target organ
```

**Pattern: ACh → ACh**

## Sympathetic pathway

```text
Thoracic/lumbar spinal cord
  ↓
Preganglionic neuron
  ↓ ACh
Sympathetic chain or collateral ganglion
  ↓
Postganglionic neuron
  ↓ usually NE
Target organ
```

**Pattern: ACh → usually norepinephrine**

## Adrenal medulla exception

```text
Thoracic/lumbar spinal cord
  ↓
Preganglionic sympathetic neuron
  ↓ ACh
Adrenal medulla
  ↓ epinephrine + norepinephrine into blood
Many target organs
```

The adrenal medulla acts like a modified sympathetic ganglion. There is no ordinary postganglionic axon extending from it to one target organ.

## High-yield corrections

- Acetylcholine does **not** transform into norepinephrine or epinephrine.
- Most sympathetic postganglionic neurons release **norepinephrine**, not epinephrine.
- Epinephrine primarily comes from the **adrenal medulla** and travels through the bloodstream.
- The **dorsal root ganglion** is a sensory ganglion, not the autonomic relay ganglion.
- Saltatory conduction occurs between **nodes of Ranvier** on myelinated axons, not at gap junctions.
- Neurotransmitter vesicles are on the **presynaptic** side.
- Neurotransmitter receptors are on the **postsynaptic** side.

## CNS/PNS naming map

| Structure grouped | CNS term | PNS term |
|---|---|---|
| Neuron cell bodies | Nucleus/nuclei | Ganglion/ganglia |
| Axons | Tract | Nerve |

Historical exception: the **basal ganglia** are CNS structures and are more accurately considered basal nuclei.

## One-line rule

**Pre/post-synaptic = role at a synapse. Preganglionic/postganglionic = position relative to a ganglion.**
